# Powerlifting Discipline Guide: Competition Lifting & Strength Development

**Discipline:** Powerlifting (Squat, Bench Press, Deadlift)  
**Sources:** NSCA, IPF (International Powerlifting Federation), Strength & Conditioning Journal  
**Focus:** Maximum strength on three competition lifts + technique precision

---

## The Three Lifts

### Squat
- **Competition depth:** Hip crease below knee level
- **Grip:** Shoulder-width or slightly wider
- **Stance:** Varies (narrow 45-75°, wide 75-90°+)
- **Key point:** Drive through entire foot; maintain upright torso

### Bench Press
- **Hand placement:** Shoulder-width or slightly wider (1.5x shoulder)
- **Feet:** Flat on platform; drive legs to stabilize
- **Arch:** Legal arch encouraged (increases range of motion advantage)
- **Pause:** 1 second minimum at chest (required in competition)

### Deadlift
- **Stance:** Hip-width to shoulder-width
- **Grip:** Conventional (overhand-underhand) or sumo (wider stance)
- **Setup:** Shins vertical; shoulders over bar; neutral spine
- **Lockout:** Full hip and knee extension; shoulders retracted

## Training Periodisation for Powerlifting

**12-week competition cycle example:**

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1-3): Hypertrophy/Volume**
- 8-12 rep range
- 60-75% 1RM
- Build work capacity
- Example: 4 × 8 @ 70% on main lift

**Phase 2 (Weeks 4-6): Strength/Intensity**
- 3-6 rep range
- 80-85% 1RM
- Increase intensity gradually
- Example: 5 × 3 @ 82% on main lift

**Phase 3 (Weeks 7-9): Peak/Competition**
- 1-3 rep range
- 85-95% 1RM
- Test maxes and competition attempts
- Example: 3 × 2 @ 88%, then 1 × 1 @ attempt

**Phase 4 (Weeks 10-12): Deload + Competition**
- Week 10: Reduce volume 50%, maintain intensity
- Week 11: Further reduce, technical work only
- Week 12: Competition

## Exercise Selection for Powerlifting

### Main Lifts (70% of volume)
- Competition Squat, Bench, Deadlift
- 60-75% of weekly sets devoted to these three

### Variations (20% volume)
Address weak points in range of motion:
- **Squat:** Pause squat, box squat, pin squat (bottom position), high-bar squat
- **Bench:** Pin press (mid-range), pause bench (chest position), close-grip bench
- **Deadlift:** Pin pull (range), rack pull (lockout), deficit deadlift (range)

### Accessory Work (10% volume)
Support compound lifts:
- Back work: Rows, pull-ups, lat pulldowns
- Core: Planks, ab wheels, cable crunches
- Leg work: Leg press, leg curls
- **Principle:** Accessories address weak links, not random arm work

## Volume for Powerlifting

**Recommended volume per lift:**
- Beginners: 4-6 sets/week per lift
- Intermediate: 6-10 sets/week per lift
- Advanced: 8-15 sets/week per lift

**Volume calculation (squat, intermediate):**
- Monday: 5 × 2 @ 90% = 5 sets
- Thursday: 3 × 5 @ 82% = 3 sets
- **Total: 8 sets/week**

**Key distinction:** Volume from heavy singles/doubles, NOT high-rep pump work.

---

## Rest Intervals

**Critical for CNS recovery:**

- **1-3 reps @ 90%+ 1RM:** 3-5 minutes (full ATP-PC recovery)
- **3-5 reps @ 85-90%:** 2-3 minutes
- **5-8 reps @ 80-85%:** 1.5-2 minutes

**Under-resting consequences:**
- 30% strength loss on subsequent sets
- Missed neural adaptations
- Increased fatigue without strength benefit

**Example (wrong approach):**
- Set 1: 3 reps @ 90% 1RM
- Wait 60 seconds
- Set 2: Only able to do 2 reps (insufficient recovery)

## Form Cues for Feedback

### Squat
✅ **Good form indicators:**
- Depth: Hip crease below parallel
- Knee tracking: Knees track over toes (no collapse)
- Torso angle: Maintains upright position (not excessive forward lean)
- Drive: Explosive up phase; controlled descent
- Leg drive: Both legs drive equally

⚠️ **Common form faults:**
- High box squat (depth insufficient; invalid competition lift)
- Knee collapse inward (valgus; form fault)
- Forward lean excessive (poor leverage; stresses lower back)
- Unequal leg drive (strength imbalance; asymmetry)

### Bench Press
✅ **Good form indicators:**
- Foot position: Feet flat, drive into platform
- Back arch: Legal arch (scapulae retracted)
- Bar path: Vertical; bar path over wrists
- Pause: Full stop at chest; 1+ second
- Lockout: Full arm extension; even arm drive

⚠️ **Common form faults:**
- Excessive butt off bench (form fault in competition)
- Uneven arm drive (strength imbalance)
- Bouncing bar (invalid lift; chest must decelerate bar)
- Elbows tucked too tight (shoulder strain; reduced power)

### Deadlift
✅ **Good form indicators:**
- Bar path: Straight vertical line; bar over mid-foot
- Shins: Vertical to slightly forward at start
- Hip hinge: Posterior chain drive (not quad-dominant)
- Lockout: Full hip/knee extension; shoulders retracted
- Grip: Secure; elbows straight (not bent)

⚠️ **Common form faults:**
- Bar away from body (increased lower back stress; inefficient)
- Hips too high (becomes a squat, not deadlift)
- Premature lockout (incomplete lift; invalid)
- Rounded back (injury risk; form fault)

## Recovery Imperatives for Powerlifting

**CNS-heavy sport; recovery is critical**

1. **Sleep:** 8-9 hours per night (CNS recovery priority)
2. **Between-session recovery:** 48-72 hours before next heavy session on same lift
3. **Deload weeks:** Every 4-6 weeks (reduce volume 40-50%)
4. **Adequate calories:** Don't diet while pursuing strength (need energy)
5. **Stress management:** High stress impairs CNS recovery

**Deload structure:**
- Reduce load by 20%
- Reduce volume by 40-50%
- Same exercises, lighter intensity
- 1 full week

## Common Mistakes in Powerlifting

**Mistake 1: Excessive Volume**
- Problem: 20+ heavy sets per lift per week; impairs recovery
- Fix: Limit to 8-15 sets per lift/week; quality over quantity

**Mistake 2: Insufficient Rest Between Sets**
- Problem: Resting 60 seconds between heavy singles; CNS incompletely recovered
- Fix: Rest 3-5 minutes between heavy doubles/singles

**Mistake 3: No Weak Point Addressing**
- Problem: Balanced training; sticking points persist
- Fix: Add 2-3 variation/accessory exercises targeting weak range

**Mistake 4: Poor Form Prioritization**
- Problem: Chasing numbers; form deteriorates; injury risk
- Fix: Video record lifts monthly; prioritize form over load

**Mistake 5: No Deload Weeks**
- Problem: Training heavy 8-12 weeks straight; accumulated fatigue
- Fix: Deload every 4-6 weeks; allows adaptation

**Mistake 6: Inadequate Accessory Work**
- Problem: Only main lifts; weak links unprepared
- Fix: 10-20% volume to accessories targeting weak points

## Feedback Priorities for Powerlifting Reviews

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- Main lifts prioritized (70% volume from squat/bench/deadlift)
- Heavy loads used (85%+ perceived 1RM for primary sets)
- Lower volume at high intensity (6-15 sets per lift/week)
- Long rest intervals (3-5 min between heavy sets)
- Progressive overload (load increased week-to-week)
- Variation exercises addressing weak points (pause, pin, deficit work)
- Deload weeks scheduled (every 4-6 weeks)
- Form focus (progressive, controlled movements)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Load too light (60-75% 1RM; insufficient neural stimulus)
- Excessive volume (20+ heavy sets per lift; recovery impaired)
- Insufficient rest (resting <2 min between heavy work)
- Missing main competition lifts (all accessory; no specificity)
- No progressive overload (same weight for weeks)
- Weak form focus (rushing reps; sacrificing technique)
- No accessory work addressing weak ranges
- Training all three lifts heavy same day (CNS overtax)

## Nutrition for Powerlifting

**Protein:** 1.6-2.0 g/kg (standard; no additional benefit above)

**Calories:** Maintenance or slight surplus (strength gains independent of muscle gain initially)

**Creatine:** 3-5 g/day (improves ATP-PC system; 2-3% strength gain potential)

**Caffeine:** 3-6 mg/kg, 30-45 min pre-lift (improves CNS function; 2-5% strength gain)

**Hydration:** 3-4 L/day minimum
