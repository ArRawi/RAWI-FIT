# Swimming Discipline Guide: Technique, Endurance & Cross-Training

**Discipline:** Swimming (Competitive & fitness swimming)  
**Sources:** ACSM, USA Swimming, swimming coaching literature  
**Focus:** Technique efficiency, aerobic capacity, low-impact training

---

## Swimming Strokes & Technique

### Freestyle (Front Crawl)
- **Kick:** 6-beat or 8-beat (alternating legs)
- **Arm stroke:** Power through catch and pull phase; relaxed recovery
- **Breathing:** Every 2-3 strokes (breath rhythm discipline)
- **Body position:** Horizontal; minimal hip rotation initially, increasing rotation with speed

### Backstroke
- **Similar to freestyle:** but on back
- **Head position:** Look straight up; avoid looking forward (increases drag)
- **Kick:** 6-beat or 8-beat
- **Recovery:** Arm comes over shoulder (not across body)

### Breaststroke
- **Kick:** Whip kick (simultaneous legs in butterfly motion)
- **Arm stroke:** Simultaneous pull and press; full extension recovery
- **Breathing:** Every stroke (breathe on press phase)
- **Lower energy:** Most efficient stroke; recovery pace ideal

### Butterfly
- **Most demanding:** Simultaneous arm recovery; dolphin kick
- **Used for:** Interval training; short efforts; skill work
- **Difficulty:** Highest technical demand; injury risk if form poor

## Weekly Training Structure (5-Day Example)

**Monday:** 2500m swim
- 400m warm-up (easy, mixed strokes)
- 8 × 300m @ moderate intensity (2 min rest)
- 400m cool-down easy

**Tuesday:** 3000m swim
- 400m warm-up
- 10 × 200m @ threshold intensity (90 sec rest)
- 500m cool-down

**Wednesday:** 2000m easy recovery
- All freestyle, easy pace
- Focus on technique; no intensity

**Thursday:** 3000m swim
- 400m warm-up
- 10 × 200m @ VO2 max intensity (60 sec rest)
- 200m cool-down

**Friday:** Rest or dryland cross-training

**Saturday:** 4000m long swim
- 400m warm-up
- 3000m continuous @ easy pace (Zone 2)
- 400m cool-down (easy)

**Sunday:** Rest or 30 min cycling/running

**Total:** 14,500m/week (80% easy/moderate, 20% hard)

## Aerobic Training Zones for Swimming

### Zone 1-2 (Easy Recovery)
- **Pace:** Comfortable; can hold conversation
- **Heart rate:** 60-75% max HR
- **Duration:** 20-90 minutes
- **Volume:** 70-80% of weekly volume

### Zone 3 (Moderate)
- **Pace:** Conversation difficult; elevated breathing
- **Heart rate:** 75-85% max HR
- **Duration:** 5-15 minutes
- **Avoid:** Most swimmers do too much Zone 3

### Zone 4 (Threshold)
- **Pace:** Hard; can only speak single words
- **Heart rate:** 85-90% max HR
- **Sets:** 4-8 × 200-400m
- **Volume:** 10-15% of weekly volume

### Zone 5 (VO2 Max)
- **Pace:** Very hard; anaerobic
- **Heart rate:** 90-100% max HR
- **Sets:** 6-10 × 100-200m
- **Volume:** 5-10% of weekly volume; 1x per week

## Technique-Focused Sessions

**Drill-Based Training (30-40% of sessions):**

**Kick Sets:**
- 6 × 75m kick (boards or dolphin)
- Builds leg strength; improves kick efficiency

**Catch-Up Drills:**
- Swimmer touches fingers to fingers before each stroke
- Forces full extension; prevents crossover

**Fingertip Drag:**
- Recovery phase: fingers drag water surface
- Encourages high recovery; efficient arm path

**Side Kick Drill:**
- 50% freestyle (one side extended)
- 50% normal freestyle (switching sides)
- Builds balance; improves rotation

## Volume & Distance Progression

### Beginner
- **Weekly volume:** 5,000-8,000 meters
- **Main set:** 200-400m repeats
- **Long swim:** 1,500-2,000m

### Intermediate
- **Weekly volume:** 8,000-15,000 meters
- **Main set:** 300-800m repeats
- **Long swim:** 2,500-4,000m

### Advanced
- **Weekly volume:** 15,000-25,000+ meters
- **Main set:** 400-1500m repeats
- **Long swim:** 4,000-6,000m+

### Long Swim Strategy
- **Duration:** Once per week
- **Pace:** Easy (Zone 2, conversational)
- **Purpose:** Aerobic base building
- **Fueling:** Drink sports drink every 20-30 min (in pool during break)

---

## Cross-Training for Swimmers

**Purpose:** Maintain fitness; prevent overuse injuries; variety

**Options:**
- **Running:** 30-45 min easy (Zone 2)
- **Cycling:** 45-60 min easy (Zone 2)
- **Elliptical:** 30-45 min easy (low impact)
- **Strength training:** 30-40 min (core, shoulder, legs)

**Frequency:** 1-2x per week (not competition-focused swimmers)

## Common Swimming Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Excessive Intensity Too Often**
- Problem: Hard intervals 3-4 days/week; no easy recovery
- Fix: 80% easy/moderate, 20% hard

**Mistake 2: Neglecting Technique**
- Problem: All distance; no drill work; technique suffers
- Fix: 30-40% of sessions are drill-focused

**Mistake 3: Insufficient Volume Build**
- Problem: Staying at 5,000m/week; fitness plateaus
- Fix: Gradually increase 5-10% every 2-3 weeks

**Mistake 4: Poor Breathing Rhythm**
- Problem: Irregular breathing; oxygen debt; limiting factor
- Fix: Practice consistent 2-3 stroke breathing pattern

**Mistake 5: Shoulder Overuse**
- Problem: Heavy training without mobility; shoulder pain
- Fix: Mobility work 15 min post-session

## Feedback Priorities for Swimming

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- Volume progression visible (gradual weekly increase)
- Technique work included (drills 30-40% of sessions)
- Zone distribution correct (80% easy, 20% hard)
- Long swim present (2000-4000m weekly)
- Cross-training included (1-2x weekly)
- Recovery days present (2-3 complete rest)
- Mobility emphasis (shoulder health)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Most sessions moderate/hard (insufficient easy swimming)
- No drill work (technique suffers; efficiency plateaus)
- Volume stagnant (no progression; fitness plateau)
- Excessive volume jumps (>10% weekly; overuse injury risk)
- No cross-training (overuse injuries; monotony)
- Insufficient recovery (swimming hard daily)
- Shoulder mobility neglected (pain risk)

## Nutrition for Swimming

**Protein:** 1.4-1.6 g/kg (endurance sport)

**Carbs:** 5-7 g/kg (high-volume training)

**Hydration:** Critical (don't become dehydrated; drink post-session)

**Timing:** Carbs + protein 30-60 min post-swim (recovery window)
