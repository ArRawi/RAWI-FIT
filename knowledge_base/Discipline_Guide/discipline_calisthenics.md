# Calisthenics Discipline Guide: Bodyweight Mastery & Skill Progressions

**Discipline:** Calisthenics (Bodyweight & gymnastic movements)  
**Sources:** GMB (Gymnastic Bodies), progressive calisthenics, skill training research  
**Focus:** Skill mastery, strength endurance, progressive variations

---

## Core Principles

**Calisthenics ≠ High-rep cardio**

- **Goal:** Master progressively harder bodyweight skills (handstands, muscle-ups, front levers)
- **Progression:** Systematic progression through variations (easier → harder)
- **Strength type:** Strength endurance (hold times, rep maximums)

---

## Progressive Skill Hierarchy

### Level 1: Foundational (Weeks 1-8)
- Push-ups (standard)
- Pull-ups (assisted or band-assisted)
- Bodyweight squats
- Planks (30-60 sec holds)
- Pike push-ups (shoulder prep)

### Level 2: Intermediate (Weeks 8-16)
- Diamond push-ups (triceps emphasis)
- Archer pull-ups (uneven load; single-arm prep)
- Bulgarian split squats (unilateral legs)
- Hollow body holds (30-60 sec)
- Handstand holds against wall (10-30 sec)

### Level 3: Advanced (Months 4-12)
- One-arm push-ups (strength endurance)
- One-arm pull-ups (advanced strength)
- Pistol squats (full ROM unilateral)
- Hollow body to handstand push-up
- Freestanding handstands (balance)

### Level 4: Expert (Years 1-2+)
- Planche progressions (front lever progression)
- Muscle-ups (transition between pull-up and dip)
- Handstand push-ups (3-5 reps)
- Lever variations (front lever hold, back lever)
- Complex combos (handstand walk, muscle-up transfers)

---

## Training Structure (3-Day Example)

**Day 1: Push Focus**
- Handstand practice: 10 min wall holds, progressions
- Push-up progression: 4 × 5-8 reps (diamond, archer, one-arm prep)
- Dip progression: 4 × 5-8 reps (if access to bar/rings)
- Mobility: 10 min

**Day 2: Pull Focus**
- Pull-up progression: 4 × 5-8 reps (archer, one-arm prep)
- Rows progression: 4 × 5-8 reps (inverted rows, ring rows)
- Lever progression: 3 × 20-30 sec holds (front lever prep, back lever)
- Mobility: 10 min

**Day 3: Full Body/Conditioning**
- Legs: Pistol squat progressions or step-ups
- Core: L-sit holds or hollow body progressions
- Cardio: 10-15 min zone 2 cardio (optional)
- Mobility: 15 min

**Rest days:** 2-3 per week (recovery critical for skill work)

## Progression Methods

### Method 1: Regression to Progression
Move to easier variation if current variation causes form breakdown.

Example (one-arm push-up progression):
- Can't do 5 archer push-ups? Drop to diamond push-ups
- Once strong at diamond, progress back to archer
- Gradual progression toward goal

### Method 2: Isometric Hold Progressions
Build strength in bottom position before full reps.

Example (front lever):
- Week 1-2: Front lever tuck holds (20-30 sec)
- Week 3-4: Single leg front lever tuck (15-20 sec)
- Week 5-6: One leg front lever full (10-15 sec)
- Week 7+: Front lever hold (5+ sec)

### Method 3: Pause Reps
Add pause at hardest point to build strength.

Example (push-up):
- 1 second pause at bottom of each rep
- Forces eccentric control; increases difficulty

### Method 4: Explosive Reps
Explosive concentric phase builds power.

Example (pull-up):
- Explosive pull-up; controlled descent
- Builds momentum for muscle-up transition

## Key Calisthenics Movements

### Handstand
- Foundational: Handstand holds against wall (10-30 sec)
- Intermediate: Handstand walk (10-30 meters)
- Advanced: Freestanding handstands (30+ sec)
- Expert: Handstand push-ups (3-5 reps)

### Planche/Front Lever
- Foundation: Hollow body hold (30-60 sec)
- Progression: Tuck planche hold (10-20 sec)
- Intermediate: Single leg planche (5-10 sec)
- Advanced: Planche hold (2-5 sec)

### Muscle-Up
- Foundation: Explosive pull-ups + dip holds
- Progression: Chest-to-bar pull-ups; ring dips
- Intermediate: Ring muscle-up (rings easier than bar)
- Advanced: Bar muscle-up (most difficult)

### L-Sit
- Foundation: Tuck L-sit (20-30 sec)
- Intermediate: Single leg L-sit (15-20 sec)
- Advanced: Full L-sit (10-15 sec)
- Expert: L-sit to handstand transitions

## Volume & Frequency

**Session duration:** 45-60 minutes

**Structure:**
- Skill work: 20-25 minutes (focused, low reps, high quality)
- Strength/conditioning: 20-25 minutes (higher reps, higher intensity)
- Mobility: 10-15 minutes (flexibility & recovery)

**Weekly frequency:** 3-5 days (depends on experience)

**Rest between skill sets:** 2-3 minutes (CNS recovery for complex movements)

---

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Excessive Volume Too Soon**
- Problem: 4 skill progressions daily; form breakdown; injury
- Fix: 1-2 skill progressions per session; quality over quantity

**Mistake 2: Neglecting Progression Regression**
- Problem: Forcing harder variation before ready; form suffers
- Fix: Drop to easier variation; perfect form first

**Mistake 3: Insufficient Mobility**
- Problem: Limited shoulder/wrist/hip mobility; movement restrictions
- Fix: 15-20 min daily mobility work

**Mistake 4: No Strength Endurance Work**
- Problem: Only skill practice; insufficient rep capacity
- Fix: Add 20% of volume as high-rep conditioning

**Mistake 5: Inadequate Recovery**
- Problem: Skill work daily; CNS fatigue; plateau
- Fix: 2-3 rest days per week; adequate sleep

---

## Feedback Priorities for Calisthenics

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- Skill progressions systematic (clear progression path)
- Movement quality emphasized (full ROM, controlled negatives)
- Adequate recovery between sessions (2-3 rest days)
- Progressive overload visible (holds increasing, reps increasing)
- Mobility work included (10+ minutes)
- Adequate food intake (support bodyweight + progressions)
- Sleep emphasized (8+ hours)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Random skill selection (no clear progression path)
- Form breakdown (sacrificing form for harder variation)
- Excessive daily training (CNS fatigue; plateaus)
- Insufficient mobility (restricted ROM)
- No progression markers (can't tell if improving)
- Inadequate nutrition (energy insufficient for progressions)
- Sleep <7 hours (recovery bottleneck)

---

## Nutrition for Calisthenics

**Protein:** 1.6-1.8 g/kg (muscle preservation; skill work is CNS-heavy)

**Calories:** Maintenance (skill work doesn't require surplus like hypertrophy)

**Carbs:** 3-4 g/kg (support explosive skill work)

**Creatine:** 3-5 g/day (improves power output for explosive progressions)
