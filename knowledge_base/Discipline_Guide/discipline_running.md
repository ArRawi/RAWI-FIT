# Running Discipline Guide: Aerobic Development & Distance Training

**Discipline:** Running (Road running, track, marathon)  
**Sources:** ACSM, running coaching literature, peer-reviewed research  
**Focus:** Aerobic base, VO2 max, lactate threshold, injury prevention

---

## Running Gait & Form

### Cadence (Steps per minute)
- **Optimal cadence:** 170-180 steps per minute (for most runners)
- **Too slow (<160 spm):** Increased ground contact time; injury risk
- **Too fast (>190 spm):** Excessive energy cost
- **Action:** If below 170 spm, increase by 5-10 spm over 3-4 weeks

### Foot Strike
- **Midfoot strike:** Optimal (foot lands under center of mass)
- **Forefoot strike:** High cadence; more impact
- **Heel strike:** Higher injury risk; excessive braking

### Posture
- **Slight forward lean:** From ankles, not waist
- **Upright posture:** Shoulders relaxed; chest open
- **Arm swing:** 90° elbow bend; forward-backward motion (not side-to-side)

## Weekly Training Structure (5-Day Example)

**Monday:** Rest or 4 km easy (Zone 1)

**Tuesday:** 10 km with 4 × 3 min @ Zone 4 (threshold work)
- 2 km warm-up
- 4 × 3 min @ Zone 4, 2 min easy between
- 2 km cool-down

**Wednesday:** 6 km easy (Zone 2)

**Thursday:** 10 km with 5 × 2 min @ Zone 5 (VO2 max intervals)
- 2 km warm-up
- 5 × 2 min @ Zone 5, 2 min easy between
- 2 km cool-down

**Friday:** Rest or mobility

**Saturday:** 14 km long run (Zone 2)

**Sunday:** Rest

**Total:** 40 km/week (80% Zone 2, 20% hard)

---

## Long Run Strategy

### Distance Progression
- **Beginner:** 45-60 min Zone 2
- **Intermediate:** 90-120 min Zone 2
- **Advanced:** 120-180+ min Zone 2

### Fueling for Long Runs
- **Under 90 min:** Water only
- **90-120 min:** 30-60g carbs per hour (gels, sports drink)
- **120+ min:** 60-90g carbs per hour

### Timing
- Once per week
- Friday or Saturday
- Allow 5-7 days before next hard session

## Zone-Based Weekly Breakdown

**80/20 Polarized Training:**

- **80% Zone 1-2:** 32 km easy/recovery
  - Easy runs: 6-10 km
  - Recovery runs: 4-6 km
  - Long run: 12-16 km

- **20% Zone 4-5:** 8 km hard
  - Threshold session: 4-6 km total hard
  - VO2 max session: 5-8 min total hard

## Injury Prevention (Critical)

### The 10% Rule
- Maximum weekly mileage increase: 10% per week
- Example: 40 km → 44 km (not 48 km)
- Violation consequences: 30-50% injury rate

### Rest Weeks
- Every 3-4 weeks: Reduce mileage 40-50%
- Example: 40 km → 20-24 km
- Purpose: Prevent cumulative overuse injuries

### Cross-Training
- 1 day per week: Cycling, swimming, elliptical (low-impact)
- Maintains fitness; reduces running impact
- Example: Replace Thursday run with 30 min cycling

### Mobility & Strength
- 15-20 min: Daily running-specific drills
- Hip strengthening: Clamshells, lateral walks, step-ups
- Core work: Planks, dead bugs, pallof presses
- Calf/ankle mobility: Foam rolling, stretching

## Common Running Injuries & Prevention

**Plantar Fasciitis:**
- Cause: Inadequate arch support; excessive mileage increase
- Prevention: Gradual mileage increase; calf stretching; proper shoes

**IT Band Syndrome:**
- Cause: Tight IT band; inadequate lateral hip strength
- Prevention: Hip strengthening; foam rolling; reduce mileage increase rate

**Shin Splints:**
- Cause: Excessive impact; inadequate recovery
- Prevention: Gradual mileage build; rest weeks; cross-training

**Runner's Knee:**
- Cause: Weak glutes; poor form; excessive mileage
- Prevention: Glute strengthening; form check; deload weeks

## Running Metrics & Progression

### Resting Heart Rate (RHR)
- Track daily
- Should decrease 1-2 bpm per month with training
- Elevation indicates inadequate recovery

### Pace at Same Heart Rate
- Track pace at Zone 2 heart rate
- As fitness improves, pace faster at same HR
- Example: 9:30/km @ 150 bpm → 9:00/km @ 150 bpm (improvement)

### Lactate Threshold Pace
- Run test: 10 min warm-up + 30 min max effort + 5 min cool-down
- Pace during 30 min = approximately lactate threshold pace
- Use for threshold workout intensity
- Test every 6-8 weeks

### VO2 Max Estimate
- 1-mile time trial (max effort, 5 min recovery first)
- Use calculator or Garmin watch
- Should improve 2-5% per 8 weeks with training

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Training Too Hard Too Often**
- Problem: Zone 3 "moderate" most sessions; accumulated fatigue
- Fix: 80% Zone 2, 20% Zone 4-5; almost no Zone 3

**Mistake 2: Excessive Mileage Increase**
- Problem: +15-20% per week; overuse injuries
- Fix: Maximum 10% per week; deload every 3-4 weeks

**Mistake 3: Insufficient Long Run**
- Problem: No extended Zone 2 training
- Fix: One 90-120 min Zone 2 session per week

**Mistake 4: No Strength Training**
- Problem: Running only; weak glutes; injury risk
- Fix: Hip/core strengthening 2x per week

**Mistake 5: Poor Recovery**
- Problem: Running hard every day; CNS fatigue
- Fix: 2-3 complete rest days per week

## Feedback Priorities for Running

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- 80%+ volume in Zone 1-2 (easy)
- One dedicated threshold session (Zone 4)
- One VO2 max session (Zone 5, once/week)
- Long run present (90+ min, once/week)
- Mileage progression gradual (10% per week max)
- Rest/recovery days present (2-3 per week)
- Strength training included (2x per week)
- Deload weeks scheduled

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Most sessions moderate (Zone 3; wrong intensity distribution)
- Multiple hard sessions without recovery (overtraining)
- Long run absent or too short (<60 min)
- Mileage jumps excessive (>10% per week)
- No strength training (injury risk)
- Insufficient rest (training 6-7 days/week)
- RHR elevated (recovery inadequate)
- No periodisation visible

## Nutrition for Running

**Protein:** 1.4-1.6 g/kg (lower than resistance training)

**Carbs:** 5-7 g/kg (high volume running requires high carbs)

**Hydration:** 3-4 L per day baseline + additional during/after runs

**Timing:** Carbs 1-2 hours pre-run; carbs + protein 30-60 min post-run

**Key takeaway:**
> Running fitness builds on easy miles. Follow 80/20: mostly easy, infrequent hard. Gradual mileage progression prevents injuries. Patience over years builds aerobic capacity and racing speed.
