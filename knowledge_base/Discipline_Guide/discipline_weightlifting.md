# Weightlifting (Olympic) Discipline Guide: Explosive Power & Technical Mastery

**Discipline:** Olympic Weightlifting (Snatch, Clean & Jerk)  
**Sources:** IWF (International Weightlifting Federation), NSCA, peer-reviewed research  
**Focus:** Maximum explosive power + Technical precision + Mobility

---

## The Two Competition Lifts

### Snatch
- **Movement:** Explosive pull to overhead position in one continuous motion
- **Phases:** Setup → Pull → Transition (turnover) → Dip/Recovery (squat or power position)
- **Grip:** Wider than shoulder-width (typically 1.5x shoulder)
- **Load range:** 70-85% of Clean & Jerk max

### Clean & Jerk
- **Movement:** Two-phase lift (clean to shoulder, then jerk overhead)
- **Clean phases:** Setup → Pull → Transition → Squat/Power Recovery
- **Jerk phases:** Dip → Drive → Turnover (catch arms overhead) → Recovery (split or squat)
- **Grip:** Shoulder-width
- **Load range:** 90-105% of Snatch max (typically heavier)

---

## Technical Proficiency Requirements

### Movement Complexity
- Snatch: 8-10 distinct technical phases
- Clean & Jerk: 12-14 distinct technical phases
- **Implication:** Requires deliberate practice; movement quality > volume

### Mobility Demands
- **Ankle mobility:** Dorsiflexion needed for comfortable squat bottom
- **Hip mobility:** Internal/external rotation for wide squat position
- **Thoracic mobility:** Extension for overhead position
- **Wrist mobility:** Extension for front/overhead rack position
- **Shoulder mobility:** Overhead stability + full range of motion

**Mobility work:** 15-20 minutes daily; part of warm-up and cool-down

## Training Structure for Olympic Lifting

### Session Structure

**1. Warm-up (15 minutes)**
- General: Light cardio, dynamic mobility
- Specific: Snatch/clean progression drills with empty bar

**2. Technical Work (30-45 minutes)**
- Competition lift focus: Snatch or Clean & Jerk
- Progression: Tall snatches → Power snatches → Snatches
- Volume: 15-25 reps of main lift
- Intensity: 60-85% 1RM (focus on movement quality)
- Rest: 2-3 minutes between sets (CNS recovery for explosive work)

**3. Strength/Power Accessory (20-30 minutes)**
- Squat variations, deadlift variations, pressing
- Volume: 4-8 sets per exercise
- Intensity: 70-85% 1RM

**4. Conditioning/Accessory (10-15 minutes)**
- Core work, isolation, mobility
- Light intensity; recovery focus

**Total session:** 90-120 minutes (includes mobility breaks)

## Weekly Training Frequency

**Typical Olympic weightlifter schedule:**
- 4-5 training days per week
- 2 heavy technique days (Snatch focus + Clean & Jerk focus)
- 1-2 light technique days (light volume, high quality)
- 1 accessory/strength day

**Example week:**

**Monday:** Heavy Snatch Day
- Snatch technique work: 4-5 × 1-2 @ 80-85%
- Accessory: Front squats, mobility

**Tuesday:** Heavy Clean & Jerk Day
- Clean & Jerk technique: 4-5 × 1-2 @ 80-85%
- Accessory: Back squats, pressing

**Wednesday:** Light Snatch Day
- Snatch work: 3-4 × 2-3 @ 70%
- Mobility focus

**Thursday:** Strength/Accessory Day
- Deadlifts, strict press, core work
- Higher reps (6-10 range), lower intensity

**Friday:** Light Clean & Jerk Day
- Clean & Jerk work: 3-4 × 2-3 @ 70%
- Mobility

**Saturday-Sunday:** Rest or light mobility

## Technical Progression (Beginner to Advanced)

### Beginner Phase (Months 1-3)
- **Focus:** Movement fundamentals; light loads (50-60% estimated max)
- **Volume:** 15-20 reps per lift per session
- **Frequency:** 2x per week per lift
- **Priority:** Mobility + technique > load

### Intermediate Phase (Months 4-12)
- **Focus:** Load progression; technique refinement; 65-75% max
- **Volume:** 20-30 reps per lift per session
- **Frequency:** 2-3x per week per lift (one heavy, one light)
- **Priority:** Movement quality with moderate load

### Advanced Phase (Years 1-3+)
- **Focus:** Peak performance; 80-95% max attempts
- **Volume:** 15-25 reps per lift per session
- **Frequency:** 2-3x per week per lift
- **Priority:** Technical precision + maximum load

## Common Technical Faults & Fixes

### Snatch Faults

**Fault: Bar away from body (mid-pull)**
- Consequence: Loss of power; bar harder to catch
- Fix: Focus on "tall snatches" (jump + pull); practice bar position drills

**Fault: Early arm bend (pulling with arms too early)**
- Consequence: Reduced power transfer; bar drops
- Fix: Pull snatch (no squat); focus on leg extension before arm bend

**Fault: Poor turnover (slow transition from pull to catch)**
- Consequence: Bar crashes on shoulders; miss opportunity
- Fix: Power snatch + overhead squat; practice aggressive catching

**Fault: Unstable overhead position**
- Consequence: Miss lock-out; wobbling
- Fix: Snatch press-outs; overhead squats; wrist/shoulder mobility

### Clean & Jerk Faults

**Fault: Incomplete clean pull (early lockout)**
- Consequence: Power loss; bar heavy; difficult jerk
- Fix: Tall cleans; hang cleans; focus on full leg extension

**Fault: Poor jerk dip (too wide or too narrow)**
- Consequence: Unbalanced; load misaligned; missed lift
- Fix: Jerk dip squats; pause in dip position; dip mechanics drills

**Fault: Incomplete jerk drive (soft push)**
- Consequence: Insufficient height for catch; arm bend compromise
- Fix: Power jerk; push press; focus on leg drive explosiveness

## Accessory Exercises for Olympic Lifting

### Squat Variations
- **Front squat:** Directly supports clean catch position
- **Back squat:** Posterior chain strength; overall lower body
- **Overhead squat:** Snatch catch position practice
- Volume: 4-6 sets × 3-5 reps @ 75-85% 1RM

### Deadlift Variations
- **Conventional deadlift:** Posterior chain power
- **Deficit deadlift:** Extended range; more pull development
- **Hang power clean:** Explosive pull development
- Volume: 3-5 sets × 2-3 reps @ 80-85% 1RM

### Pressing Variations
- **Strict press (standing):** Overhead strength
- **Push press:** Leg involvement; power transfer
- **Jerk dip:** Jerk-specific movement
- Volume: 3-5 sets × 3-5 reps @ 70-80% 1RM

### Supplementary
- **Bent-over rows:** Back strength
- **Pull-ups:** Upper back development
- **Core work:** Planks, anti-rotation holds
- Volume: 2-3 sets × 6-10 reps per exercise

## Recovery & Injury Prevention

### Mobility Work (Non-Negotiable)
- **Daily:** 15-20 minutes mobility (ankle, hip, thoracic, shoulder, wrist)
- **Post-session:** 10-15 minutes specific mobility

### Sleep
- 8-9 hours per night (explosive CNS work requires full recovery)

### Deload Weeks
- Every 3-4 weeks: Reduce volume 40-50%, maintain technical quality

### Joint Health
- Emphasis on form over load (joint stress high in Olympic lifting)
- Adequate warm-up (prevent acute injury)
- Mobility maintenance (prevent chronic issues)

## Feedback Priorities for Olympic Weightlifting Reviews

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- Snatch & Clean & Jerk technique work prioritized (60-70% of session)
- Technical progression visible (light to heavy pyramid)
- Movement quality emphasized (controlled positions, no rushing)
- Adequate mobility work (15+ minutes daily)
- Accessory work supports competition lifts (squats, deadlifts, pressing)
- Progressive overload in place (load increases from week to week)
- Deload weeks scheduled every 3-4 weeks
- Recovery emphasis (sleep, mobility, stress management)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Load too heavy too soon (sacrificing technical quality)
- Insufficient mobility work (limited mobility progress)
- Excessive volume (25+ reps per lift per session is too much early)
- Poor movement quality (sloppy positioning; speed rushing form)
- No accessory work (all Olympic lifting; lacks supporting strength)
- Training same lift 4-5 days/week (inadequate recovery; CNS fatigue)
- Missing deload weeks (technical fatigue accumulates)
- No progression plan visible (random loads)

## Nutrition for Olympic Weightlifting

**Protein:** 1.8-2.0 g/kg (hypertrophy support + recovery)

**Calories:** Slight surplus optimal (need energy for power development)

**Carbohydrates:** 5-7 g/kg (explosive work highly glycogen-dependent)

**Timing:** Carbs 1-2 hours pre-session (fuels explosive work)

**Creatine:** 3-5 g/day (improves power output ~5%)
