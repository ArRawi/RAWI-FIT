# General Fitness Discipline Guide: Balanced Health & Wellness

**Discipline:** General Fitness (Balanced approach, not specialized)  
**Sources:** WHO, ACSM, CDC, peer-reviewed health research  
**Focus:** Overall health, WHO compliance, sustainable lifestyle, long-term wellness

---

## WHO Physical Activity Guidelines

**For adults 18-64 years (applies to "General Fitness"):**

### Minimum Requirements:
- **Aerobic activity:** 150 minutes moderate-intensity OR 75 minutes vigorous-intensity per week
- **Strength training:** 2 days per week (all major muscle groups)
- **Flexibility:** Daily or 3-4x per week

### Optimal Health:
- **Aerobic activity:** 300 minutes moderate-intensity OR 150 minutes vigorous-intensity per week
- **Strength training:** 2-3 days per week
- **Flexibility/mobility:** Daily
- **Other:** Balance training (especially 65+)

## Weekly General Fitness Structure

### Option A: Balanced Week (5-6 days)

**Monday:** Strength Training (45-60 min)
- Warm-up (5 min)
- Compound lifts: Squat, bench, rows (12-15 sets total)
- Mobility cool-down (5-10 min)

**Tuesday:** Cardio (40-50 min)
- Zone 2 running, cycling, or swimming (easy pace)
- OR elliptical/rowing machine (moderate intensity)

**Wednesday:** Strength Training (45-60 min)
- Warm-up (5 min)
- Compound lifts: Deadlift, OHP, rows (12-15 sets total)
- Mobility cool-down (5-10 min)

**Thursday:** Flexibility/Mobility (30 min)
- Yoga, stretching, foam rolling
- OR active recovery walk

**Friday:** Cardio (40-50 min)
- Zone 2 cardio (similar to Tuesday)
- Can be different modality (if ran Tue, bike Fri)

**Saturday:** Optional Activity (30-60 min)
- Sports, recreational activity, or light hiking
- OR complete rest day

**Sunday:** Rest Day
- Complete recovery; meal prep; stress management

**Weekly totals:**
- Strength: 90-120 minutes (exceeds WHO minimum)
- Cardio: 80-100 minutes (meets WHO optimal)
- Flexibility: 30+ minutes (exceeds recommendation)

## Movement Pattern Balance

**Ensure all patterns trained 1-2x per week:**

### Push Patterns
- Horizontal push: Bench press, push-ups
- Vertical push: Overhead press, pike push-ups
- Frequency: 2x per week

### Pull Patterns
- Horizontal pull: Rows, seal rows
- Vertical pull: Pull-ups, lat pulldowns
- Frequency: 2x per week

### Lower Body
- Squat (quad-dominant): Back squat, leg press
- Hinge (posterior chain): Deadlifts, good mornings
- Frequency: 2x per week

### Core/Anti-Rotation
- Planks, pallof presses, ab wheels
- Frequency: 2x per week (every session)

## Rep Range & Volume for General Fitness

**Goal:** Build muscle, maintain strength, support metabolic health

**Rep range distribution:**
- 60% of volume: 6-12 reps (hypertrophy + strength balance)
- 40% of volume: 12-15+ reps (muscular endurance + metabolic)

**Volume targets per session:**
- Strength session: 12-16 sets total
- Cardio session: 40-50 minutes moderate intensity
- Flexibility session: 30+ minutes

## Cardiovascular Training Options

### Zone-Based Training
- **Zone 2 (Easy):** 60-70% max HR; conversational pace
- **Zone 3 (Moderate):** 70-80% max HR; can speak in phrases
- **Zone 4 (Hard):** 80-90% max HR; difficult to speak

**Distribution for general fitness:**
- 70% Zone 2 (easy, sustainable)
- 20% Zone 3 (moderate)
- 10% Zone 4 (hard, infrequent)

### Modality Options
- **Running:** Accessible; high impact
- **Cycling:** Low impact; joint-friendly
- **Swimming:** Zero impact; total body
- **Rowing:** Full body engagement; low impact
- **Elliptical:** Low impact; beginner-friendly
- **Walking:** Most accessible; low intensity

**Recommendation:** Mix modalities to prevent overuse injuries.

## Progressive Overload

**For general fitness (not sport-specific):**

1. **Add weight:** +2.5-5 kg every 1-3 weeks on main lifts
2. **Add reps:** If can't add weight, add reps
3. **Add duration:** Increase cardio duration 5-10 min every 2-3 weeks
4. **Improve form:** Better ROM, more controlled movement

**Example (Squat):**
- Week 1: 3 × 8 @ 80 kg
- Week 2: 3 × 9 @ 80 kg (added rep)
- Week 3: 3 × 8 @ 82.5 kg (reset reps, added weight)

## Flexibility & Mobility Work

**Non-negotiable for long-term health**

**Daily routine (10-15 min):**
- Arm circles, leg swings
- Cat-cow stretches
- Hip flexor stretch
- Hamstring stretch
- Thoracic rotations
- Wrist mobility
- Ankle mobility

**Weekly dedicated session (30+ min):**
- Yoga, formal stretching routine, or foam rolling
- Focus on problem areas (tight hips, shoulders, etc.)

## Nutrition for General Fitness

### Protein
- **Target:** 1.4-1.6 g/kg bodyweight
- **Why:** Maintains muscle; supports metabolic health
- **Example (70 kg person):** 98-112g protein daily

### Carbohydrates
- **Target:** 3-4 g/kg (depending on activity level)
- **Example (70 kg person):** 210-280g carbs daily
- **Timing:** Include with all meals; more around training

### Fats
- **Target:** 0.8-1.2 g/kg
- **Example (70 kg person):** 56-84g fat daily
- **Types:** Include saturated, monounsaturated, polyunsaturated

### Hydration
- **Baseline:** 3-4 liters daily
- **During exercise:** 500 mL per 30 min activity
- **Post-exercise:** 1.5 L per 1 kg bodyweight lost

### Micronutrients
- Varied whole foods ensure micronutrient intake
- Consider vitamin D if limited sun exposure
- Consider B12 if vegan/vegetarian

## Common Mistakes in General Fitness

**Mistake 1: Specializing Too Early**
- Problem: Only runs, only lifts; missing balanced health
- Fix: Include both strength + cardio + flexibility

**Mistake 2: Insufficient Recovery**
- Problem: Training 6-7 days/week; overtraining
- Fix: 2-3 complete rest days per week

**Mistake 3: Inadequate Sleep**
- Problem: 6 hours/night; impacts recovery and metabolism
- Fix: Prioritize 7-9 hours consistent sleep

**Mistake 4: Ignoring Movement Quality**
- Problem: Form sacrificed for speed/weight; injury risk
- Fix: Perfect form at moderate intensity; load follows

**Mistake 5: Neglecting Mobility**
- Problem: No stretching/flexibility work; restricted ROM
- Fix: Include 15-20 min daily mobility work

## Feedback Priorities for General Fitness

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- All movement patterns trained (push, pull, squat, hinge, core)
- Strength training 2-3 days/week (WHO compliance)
- Cardio 150+ min/week of moderate intensity (WHO optimal)
- Flexibility work included (30+ min weekly)
- Progressive overload visible (load or duration increasing)
- Recovery prioritized (2-3 rest days, 7+ hours sleep)
- Balanced macronutrition (protein 1.4+, carbs 3+, fat adequate)
- Sustainable, enjoyable activities (long-term adherence focus)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Unbalanced training (only strength or only cardio)
- Insufficient strength training (<90 min/week)
- Inadequate cardio (<150 min/week at moderate intensity)
- No flexibility work (mobility restricted)
- Excessive training frequency (6-7 days/week; overtraining)
- Sleep <7 hours/night (recovery bottleneck)
- Protein <1.4 g/kg (insufficient for muscle preservation)
- Training becoming unsustainable (not enjoying activities)

## Long-Term Health Markers to Track

**Every 3-6 months:**

1. **Resting Heart Rate:** Should decrease 1-2 bpm with training
2. **Body Composition:** Ideally: maintain/increase muscle, decrease fat
3. **Performance:** Strength (can you lift more?), endurance (faster pace?)
4. **Mobility:** Can you touch your toes? Overhead reach?
5. **Blood Pressure:** Should be <120/80 mmHg
6. **Energy Levels:** Feel more energized daily?
7. **Sleep Quality:** Sleeping better?
8. **Mood:** Feel more positive overall?

---

## Sustainability & Adherence

**Keys to long-term success:**

1. **Find enjoyable activities** (you'll continue longer)
2. **Train with others** (community increases adherence)
3. **Set realistic goals** (progress compounds over years)
4. **Track progress** (visible improvement motivates)
5. **Adjust when bored** (variety maintains engagement)
6. **Prioritize consistency** (regular > intense & irregular)
7. **Manage stress** (rest & recovery are training)
8. **Celebrate wins** (acknowledge progress)
