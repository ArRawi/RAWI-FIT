# Cycling Discipline Guide: Power Development & Endurance Training

**Discipline:** Cycling (Road cycling, competitive, endurance)  
**Sources:** TrainingPeaks, cycling coaching literature, ACSM  
**Focus:** Power zones, aerobic base, VO2 max, sustainable pace

---

## Power-Based Training Zones

### Zone 1 (Active Recovery)
- **Power:** <55% FTP (Functional Threshold Power)
- **Heart rate:** 50-60% max HR
- **Effort:** Very easy; recovery rides
- **Duration:** 30-90 minutes

### Zone 2 (Aerobic Base)
- **Power:** 56-75% FTP
- **Heart rate:** 60-70% max HR
- **Effort:** Comfortable; can hold conversation
- **Duration:** 60-180 minutes

### Zone 3 (Tempo)
- **Power:** 76-90% FTP
- **Heart rate:** 70-80% max HR
- **Effort:** Moderate; conversation difficult
- **Avoid:** Most cyclists spend too much time here

### Zone 4 (Lactate Threshold)
- **Power:** 91-105% FTP
- **Heart rate:** 80-90% max HR
- **Effort:** Hard; breathing elevated
- **Duration:** 20-45 minutes (intervals or continuous)

### Zone 5 (VO2 Max)
- **Power:** 106-120% FTP
- **Heart rate:** 90-95% max HR
- **Effort:** Very hard; sustainable 5-10 minutes
- **Duration:** 3-8 minute intervals

### Zone 6 (Anaerobic Capacity)
- **Power:** 121-150% FTP
- **Heart rate:** 95-100% max HR
- **Effort:** All-out; 1-3 minute efforts
- **Duration:** 30 seconds - 3 minutes (sprints)

### Zone 7 (Neuromuscular Power)
- **Power:** >150% FTP
- **Heart rate:** Max HR
- **Effort:** Maximum all-out effort
- **Duration:** 5-30 seconds (sprints)

## Functional Threshold Power (FTP) Testing

**FTP:** Maximum sustainable power for 1 hour

**Test Protocol:**
- 15 min warm-up (progressive intensity)
- 5 min hard effort (builds into test)
- 10 min recovery (moderate intensity)
- 20 min max effort (measured)
- Average power during 20 min = FTP estimate
- **Formula:** 20-min average power × 0.95 = FTP

**Example:**
- 20-min average: 300 watts
- FTP = 300 × 0.95 = 285 watts

**Test frequency:** Every 6-8 weeks (track fitness progression)

## Weekly Training Structure (5-Day Example)

**Monday:** 1-hour easy ride (Zone 2)

**Tuesday:** 1.5-hour threshold session
- 20 min warm-up
- 2 × 15 min @ Zone 4 (threshold), 5 min easy between
- 15 min cool-down

**Wednesday:** 1-hour easy recovery (Zone 1-2)

**Thursday:** 1-hour VO2 max session
- 20 min warm-up
- 5 × 4 min @ Zone 5, 2 min easy between
- 15 min cool-down

**Friday:** Rest or 45 min easy spin

**Saturday:** 2-3 hour long ride (Zone 2, endurance)

**Sunday:** Rest

**Total:** 7-8 hours/week (80% Zone 1-2, 20% Zone 4+)

## Long Ride Strategy

### Distance Progression
- **Beginner:** 60-90 minutes Zone 2
- **Intermediate:** 2-3 hours Zone 2
- **Advanced:** 3-5+ hours Zone 2

### Fueling Strategy
- **Under 90 min:** Water only
- **90-180 min:** 30-60g carbs per hour (gels, bars, sports drink)
- **180+ min:** 60-90g carbs per hour (food allows higher intake)

### Pacing
- Start conservatively
- Maintain consistent Zone 2 pace
- Don't surge; train aerobic capacity, not anaerobic

## Training Intensities Distribution (80/20 Rule)

**Weekly volume breakdown:**
- 80% easy (Zone 1-2): 5.5-6.5 hours
- 20% hard (Zone 4-6): 1.5-2 hours

**Zone 3 avoidance:**
- Minimal time in "moderate" intensity
- Zone 3 fatigues without sufficient stimulus
- Hard efforts should feel significantly harder than Zone 3

## Cadence & Bike Setup

### Cadence (RPM)
- **Optimal:** 90-100 RPM (for most riders)
- **Lower cadence (<85 RPM):** Higher torque; more muscle fatigue
- **Higher cadence (>110 RPM):** Higher HR; less efficient for power

### Bike Fit
- **Saddle height:** Leg extension 25-35° when pedal at bottom
- **Saddle fore/aft:** Knee over pedal spindle
- **Bar height:** Depends on discipline (road racing lower; endurance higher)
- **Importance:** Poor fit = inefficiency + injury risk

## Common Cycling Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Training Too Hard Too Often**
- Problem: Zone 3-4 most rides; accumulated fatigue
- Fix: 80% Zone 2 easy, 20% Zone 4-5 hard

**Mistake 2: Insufficient Base Building**
- Problem: Starting hard intervals before aerobic base built
- Fix: 4-6 weeks Zone 2 base before adding hard intervals

**Mistake 3: Excessive Volume Increase**
- Problem: Doubling hours per week; overtraining
- Fix: Maximum 10% weekly volume increase

**Mistake 4: Neglecting Cadence**
- Problem: Low RPM (60-70); muscle fatigue
- Fix: Maintain 90-100 RPM; practice higher cadence

**Mistake 5: Poor Nutrition During Rides**
- Problem: Bonking (hitting the wall); no carbs
- Fix: 30-60g carbs per hour for efforts >90 min

## Feedback Priorities for Cycling

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- 80%+ volume in Zone 1-2 (easy)
- One dedicated threshold session (Zone 4)
- One VO2 max session (Zone 5)
- Long ride present (2+ hours Zone 2, once/week)
- Mileage progression gradual (10% per week max)
- Rest days present (2 complete rest minimum)
- FTP testing every 6-8 weeks
- Proper pacing on long rides (not racing)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Most rides moderate/hard (Zone 3 trap)
- Multiple high-intensity sessions (insufficient recovery)
- Long ride absent or too short (<60 min)
- Volume jumps excessive (>10% weekly)
- Insufficient rest (riding 6-7 days/week)
- Cadence analysis missing (RPM important)
- No FTP baseline tracking
- Nutrition inadequate during long efforts

---

## Nutrition for Cycling

**Protein:** 1.4-1.6 g/kg (endurance sport)

**Carbs:** 5-8 g/kg (high-volume training; more than running due to better digestion)

**During ride (>90 min):** 30-90g carbs per hour (water + gels/sports drink)

**Post-ride:** Carbs + protein within 30 min (3:1 carb:protein ratio optimal)
