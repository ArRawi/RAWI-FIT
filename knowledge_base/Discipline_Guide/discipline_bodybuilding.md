# Bodybuilding Discipline Guide: Aesthetics, Symmetry & Muscle Development

**Discipline:** Bodybuilding (Aesthetic muscle development)  
**Sources:** ISSN, ACSM, Bodybuilding competitions  
**Focus:** Maximum muscle size + Symmetry + Conditioning + Aesthetics

---

## Core Philosophy

**Bodybuilding ≠ Powerlifting or CrossFit**

- **Goal:** Aesthetic muscle development (size, symmetry, proportion)
- **Not about:** Raw strength numbers or functional performance
- **Focus:** Muscle shape, striations, separation, symmetry

---

## Training Split Example (5-Day PPL)

**Push Day (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)**
- Barbell Bench: 4 × 6-8
- Incline Dumbbell: 3 × 8-10
- Machine Press: 3 × 10-12
- Lateral Raises: 3 × 12-15
- Rope Triceps: 3 × 10-12
- **Total: 16 sets**

**Pull Day (Back, Biceps)**
- Deadlift or Rows: 4 × 6-8
- Lat Pulldown: 3 × 8-10
- Barbell Rows: 3 × 8-10
- Face Pulls: 3 × 12-15
- Barbell Curls: 3 × 8-10
- Cable Curls: 3 × 12-15
- **Total: 18 sets**

**Legs**
- Barbell Squat: 4 × 6-8
- Leg Press: 3 × 8-10
- Leg Curl: 3 × 10-12
- Leg Extension: 3 × 12-15
- Calf Raises: 3 × 15-20
- **Total: 16 sets**

**Repeat:** 5 days total (Upper/Lower/Full split also viable)

---

## Volume Targets

**Per muscle group per week:** 15-25 sets

- Beginner: 15-18 sets/muscle/week
- Intermediate: 18-24 sets/muscle/week
- Advanced: 24-30+ sets/muscle/week

**Example (Chest):**
- Monday: 7 sets chest
- Friday: 7 sets chest
- Total: 14 sets/week (sufficient for intermediate)

---

## Rep Range Strategy

**Primary rep range: 8-12 reps (70% of volume)**
- Optimal for hypertrophy
- Moderate load (70-80% 1RM)
- Metabolic stress + mechanical tension

**Secondary rep ranges:**
- Heavy (6-8 reps, 20% volume): Mechanical tension
- Light (12-15 reps, 10% volume): Metabolic stress

**Example chest session:**
- Bench Press: 4 × 8 (32 reps primary range)
- Incline DB: 3 × 10 (30 reps primary range)
- Machine: 3 × 12 (36 reps primary range)
- Cable Fly: 3 × 15 (45 reps light range)
- **Total: 143 reps; mostly 8-12 range**

---

## Exercise Selection for Aesthetics

### Compound Movements (60% volume)
- Barbell bench, squat, rows, deadlifts
- Heavy loads; recruit multiple muscles
- Build overall mass

### Isolation/Machine Work (40% volume)
- Leg press, leg curls, leg extensions
- Flys, lateral raises, cable work
- Shape individual muscles; reduce joint stress
- Allow high volume without CNS fatigue

**Key:** Compounds for overall mass; isolations for muscle shape and separation.

---

## Rest Intervals & Time-Under-Tension

**Rest between sets (8-12 rep range):** 90-120 seconds

**Time-under-tension (TUT) target:** 40-70 seconds per set

**Tempo emphasis:** 2:0:3:1 (2s up, 3s down, 1s pause)
- Eccentric (lowering) phase crucial for damage + tension
- Controlled; don't let weight drop

**Example (Bench Press, 8 reps @ 75% 1RM):**
- Each rep: ~6 seconds (2s up + 3s down + 1s pause)
- 8 reps = 48 seconds TUT (optimal)

---

## Progressive Overload for Hypertrophy

**Progression priorities (in order):**

1. **Add weight:** +2.5-5 kg every 1-2 weeks
2. **Add reps:** If can't add weight, add reps until hitting target, then reset weight
3. **Add sets:** If stuck on both, add 1-2 sets per exercise
4. **Improve form/ROM:** Deeper range; fuller stretch

**Example (Incline DB Press):**
- Week 1: 3 sets × 10 reps @ 30 kg
- Week 2: 3 sets × 11 reps @ 30 kg (added rep)
- Week 3: 3 sets × 10 reps @ 32.5 kg (added weight, reset reps)
- Week 4: 3 sets × 12 reps @ 32.5 kg (added reps)

## Muscle-Specific Training Cues

### Chest Development
- **Wide grip bench:** Chest activation (narrow grip = triceps dominant)
- **Paused reps:** Emphasize chest contraction; prevent bouncing
- **Incline angle:** Upper pec development (upper chest is lagging muscle for many)
- **Machine/fly work:** Full range; chest stretch + contraction

### Back Development
- **Lat pulldown:** Wide grip activates lats (narrow = biceps)
- **Row variation:** Focus on scapular retraction (not just arm bend)
- **Face pulls:** Posterior shoulder; upper back thickness
- **Full range:** Stretch at bottom; full contraction at top

### Leg Development
- **Squat:** Primary quad builder; glutes secondary
- **Leg press:** Easier on joints; allows higher volume
- **Leg curl:** Hamstring isolation; posterior chain
- **Calf raises:** Plantarflexion at end range; full stretch

### Arm Development
- **Biceps:** Barbell curls (heavy) + cable curls (isolation)
- **Triceps:** Compound movements (dips, bench, OHP) + cable work
- **Forearms:** Wrist curls, reverse curls; often neglected

## Contest Preparation (12-16 weeks)

### Off-Season (Bulk)
- High calories (+300-500 kcal)
- High volume training (20-25 sets/muscle/week)
- Focus on mass building
- Duration: 12-20 weeks

### Pre-Contest (Cut)
- Caloric deficit (-500 kcal)
- Moderate volume (15-20 sets/muscle/week)
- Maintain strength (heavy compounds)
- Increase conditioning/cardio
- Duration: 12-16 weeks

### Final Week (Peak Week)
- Reduce glycogen storage (deplete; refuel)
- Reduce water (flush excess; taper sodium)
- Increase cardio briefly (mobilize subcutaneous water)
- Reduce training volume 50% (prevent water retention from pump)

---

## Nutrition for Bodybuilding

### Off-Season (Bulk)
- **Calories:** +300-500 kcal above maintenance
- **Protein:** 1.8-2.2 g/kg
- **Carbs:** 4-7 g/kg (fuel for high-volume training)
- **Fats:** 0.8-1.2 g/kg

### Pre-Contest (Cut)
- **Calories:** -500 kcal below maintenance
- **Protein:** 2.2-2.4 g/kg (preserve muscle; increase NEPA)
- **Carbs:** 2-4 g/kg (reduce for deficit)
- **Fats:** 0.5-0.8 g/kg (maintain hormone health)

---

## Common Mistakes in Bodybuilding

**Mistake 1: Volume Too High Too Soon**
- Problem: 25+ sets/muscle/week without adequate recovery
- Fix: Start 15 sets/muscle/week; add 2-4 sets every 4 weeks

**Mistake 2: Excessive Heavy Load**
- Problem: All 4-6 rep range; missing metabolic stress stimulus
- Fix: 70% volume at 8-12 reps; 20% at 6-8; 10% at 12-15

**Mistake 3: Neglecting Weak Points**
- Problem: Balanced training; weak muscles never catch up
- Fix: Add 3-4 extra sets to lagging muscle groups per week

**Mistake 4: Poor Nutrition Tracking**
- Problem: Eating "clean" but not hitting macros
- Fix: Track calories/protein for 1-2 weeks minimum

**Mistake 5: Excessive Cardio During Bulk**
- Problem: Cardio >5 hours/week interferes with muscle building
- Fix: Keep cardio 2-3 hours/week during off-season

## Feedback Priorities for Bodybuilding Reviews

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- Volume in target range (15-24 sets/muscle/week)
- Primary rep range 8-12 (70% of volume)
- Mix of compound + isolation exercises (60/40 ratio)
- Progressive overload visible (weight increased or reps increased)
- Time-under-tension emphasis (2-3 second eccentrics)
- Rest intervals appropriate (90-120 sec for moderate intensity)
- Protein target on track (1.8+ g/kg logged)
- Lagging muscles targeted (extra sets to weak points)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Volume too low (<12 sets/muscle/week)
- Excessive heavy (only 4-6 reps; missing metabolic stress)
- Insufficient isolation work (all compounds; no pump/separation)
- No progressive overload (same weight repeatedly)
- Inadequate protein (below 1.6 g/kg)
- Excessive cardio during bulk (interferes with gains)
- No attention to lagging muscles (balanced but not aesthetic)
- Poor ROM (half-reps; missing muscle contraction)
