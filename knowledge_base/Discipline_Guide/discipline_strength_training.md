# Strength Training Discipline Guide: Progressive Strength Development

**Discipline:** Strength Training (General strength focus, not competitive sport)  
**Sources:** NSCA, ACSM, strength training literature  
**Focus:** Compound movement mastery, progressive loading, functional strength

---

## Core Principles

**Strength Training ≠ Bodybuilding or Powerlifting**

- **Goal:** Build practical, functional strength across all movement patterns
- **Approach:** Systematic progression on fundamental lifts
- **Not about:** Competition (powerlifting) or aesthetics (bodybuilding)
- **For:** General population seeking strength, health, and performance

---

## The Big 5 Movements

### 1. Squat (Lower Body Push)
- Targets: Quads, glutes, core
- Variations: Back squat, front squat, goblet squat
- Rep range: 3-8 reps (primary strength focus)
- Volume: 3-4 sets per session

### 2. Deadlift (Full Body Pull)
- Targets: Posterior chain (glutes, hamstrings, back)
- Variations: Conventional, sumo, trap bar
- Rep range: 1-5 reps (primary strength focus)
- Volume: 2-3 sets per session

### 3. Bench Press (Upper Body Push)
- Targets: Chest, shoulders, triceps
- Variations: Barbell, dumbbell, incline
- Rep range: 3-8 reps (primary strength focus)
- Volume: 3-4 sets per session

### 4. Rows (Upper Body Pull)
- Targets: Back, biceps
- Variations: Barbell row, dumbbell row, machine row
- Rep range: 5-8 reps (primary strength focus)
- Volume: 3-4 sets per session

### 5. Overhead Press (Upper Body Push)
- Targets: Shoulders, chest, triceps, core
- Variations: Standing OHP, seated OHP, machine press
- Rep range: 5-8 reps (primary strength focus)
- Volume: 2-3 sets per session

## Weekly Training Structure (3-4 Days)

### Option A: Full Body (3x/week)
**Day 1:**
- Squat: 4 × 4-6
- Bench Press: 3 × 6-8
- Rows: 3 × 6-8
- Accessory: 2-3 exercises

**Day 2:**
- Deadlift: 3 × 2-4
- Overhead Press: 3 × 5-6
- Leg work: 3 × 8-10
- Accessory: 2-3 exercises

**Day 3:**
- Squat: 3 × 5-6 (lighter than Day 1)
- Bench Press: 3 × 5-6 (lighter than Day 1)
- Rows: 3 × 6-8
- Accessory: 2-3 exercises

### Option B: Upper/Lower (4x/week)

**Upper A (Heavy):**
- Bench Press: 4 × 4-6
- Rows: 3 × 6-8
- Accessory: 3-4 exercises

**Lower A (Heavy):**
- Squat: 4 × 4-6
- Deadlift: 2 × 3-5
- Accessory: 3-4 exercises

**Upper B (Volume):**
- Incline Bench: 3 × 8-10
- Rows: 3 × 8-10
- Accessory: 4-5 exercises

**Lower B (Volume):**
- Leg Press: 3 × 8-10
- Leg Curls: 3 × 8-10
- Accessory: 4-5 exercises

## Progressive Overload Strategy

**Progression priorities (in order):**

1. **Add weight:** +2.5-5 kg every 1-2 weeks on main lifts
2. **Add reps:** If can't add weight, add reps until hitting target
3. **Add sets:** If stuck on both, add 1 set per exercise
4. **Improve form/ROM:** Deeper range; more controlled movement

**Example (Squat progression):**
- Week 1: 4 × 5 @ 100 kg
- Week 2: 4 × 5 @ 102.5 kg (added weight)
- Week 3: 4 × 6 @ 102.5 kg (added rep)
- Week 4: 4 × 5 @ 105 kg (reset reps, added weight)

---

## Rep Range Strategy

**Different rep ranges for different adaptations:**

- **1-5 reps (80%+ 1RM):** Strength focus; heavy loads
- **6-8 reps (75-80% 1RM):** Strength-hypertrophy balance
- **8-12 reps (70-75% 1RM):** Hypertrophy focus; metabolic stress
- **12+ reps (50-70% 1RM):** Endurance; pump

**Recommended split:**
- 60% volume at 5-8 reps (primary strength stimulus)
- 40% volume at 8-12 reps (hypertrophy + secondary stimulus)

---

## Rest Intervals

**By load:**
- Heavy (1-5 reps, 80%+ 1RM): 3-5 minutes (full CNS recovery)
- Moderate (5-8 reps, 75-80%): 2-3 minutes
- Light (8-12 reps, 70-75%): 90-120 seconds

**Principle:** Heavier load needs longer rest for force production.

## Periodisation for General Strength

### Linear Periodisation (12 weeks)

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Hypertrophy/Volume**
- 8-10 reps per set
- 70-75% 1RM
- 3 sets per exercise
- Purpose: Build work capacity

**Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8): Strength/Intensity**
- 5-6 reps per set
- 80-85% 1RM
- 3-4 sets per exercise
- Purpose: Increase load

**Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12): Peak/Strength**
- 3-5 reps per set
- 85-90% 1RM
- 4-5 sets per exercise
- Test new 1RMs

**Week 12+:** Deload & repeat

## Accessory Work

**Supporting exercises (20-30% of volume):**

**For Squat:**
- Leg curls, leg extensions, calf raises
- Core: Planks, ab wheels

**For Deadlift:**
- Back work: Rows, lat pulldowns
- Leg work: Leg curls, leg press
- Core: Dead bugs, pallof presses

**For Bench Press:**
- Dumbbell bench, incline press
- Triceps: Rope extensions, dips
- Shoulders: Lateral raises

**For Rows:**
- Variations: Seal rows, machine rows
- Biceps: Curls, hammer curls
- Back: Face pulls, lat pulldowns

**Principle:** Accessories address weak links, not random arm work.

## Recovery & Deload

### Sleep
- 7-9 hours per night (CNS recovery critical for strength)

### Between-Session Recovery
- 48-72 hours before heavy session on same movement
- Example: Heavy squat Monday, light squat Thursday (3-4 days apart)

### Deload Weeks
- Every 4-6 weeks: Reduce volume 40-50%, maintain intensity
- Purpose: Reset nervous system; allow adaptation

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Insufficient Load**
- Problem: Using 60-70% 1RM; insufficient neural stimulus
- Fix: Base training at 75-85% 1RM

**Mistake 2: Excessive Volume**
- Problem: 20+ heavy sets per movement per week; impairs recovery
- Fix: Limit to 8-15 sets per movement per week

**Mistake 3: No Deload Weeks**
- Problem: Training heavy 8-12 weeks straight; accumulated fatigue
- Fix: Deload every 4-6 weeks

**Mistake 4: Poor Form Prioritization**
- Problem: Chasing heavy loads; form deteriorates
- Fix: Perfect form first; load follows naturally

**Mistake 5: Ignoring Weak Points**
- Problem: Balanced training; sticking points persist
- Fix: Identify weak range; add 2-3 specific exercises

## Feedback Priorities for Strength Training

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- Main lifts prioritized (60-70% of volume)
- Heavy loads used (75-85% perceived 1RM)
- Appropriate volume (8-15 sets per main movement/week)
- Long rest intervals (2-5 min for heavy work)
- Progressive overload visible (load increased from last week)
- Proper periodisation (phases of different rep ranges)
- Deload weeks scheduled (every 4-6 weeks)
- Recovery emphasis (sleep, recovery days)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Load too light (50-70% 1RM; insufficient stimulus)
- Excessive volume (20+ sets per movement; recovery impaired)
- Insufficient rest (resting <2 min between heavy sets)
- Missing main lifts (all accessory; no specificity)
- No progressive overload (same weight for weeks)
- No periodisation (random intensities)
- Training same lift 4+ days/week (inadequate recovery)
- Sleep <7 hours (CNS recovery bottleneck)

## Nutrition for Strength Training

**Protein:** 1.6-2.0 g/kg

**Calories:** Maintenance or slight surplus (strength gains less dependent on calories than hypertrophy)

**Carbs:** 3-4 g/kg (support training intensity)

**Creatine:** 3-5 g/day (improves ATP-PC system; 2-3% strength improvement)

**Caffeine:** 3-6 mg/kg pre-workout (improves CNS function; 2-5% strength gain)
