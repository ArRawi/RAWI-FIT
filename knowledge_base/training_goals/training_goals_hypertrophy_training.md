# Hypertrophy Training: Evidence-Based Goal-Specific Protocol

**Goal:** Maximum muscle growth through optimized training, nutrition, and recovery.

**Sources:** ISSN, ACSM, NSCA, peer-reviewed research  
**Key principles:** Mechanical tension + Metabolic stress + Muscle damage + Adequate protein + Recovery

---

## Training Volume Targets

**Weekly sets per muscle group (target range):**

- **Beginner (0-1 year training):** 10-12 sets/muscle/week
- **Intermediate (1-3 years):** 12-16 sets/muscle/week
- **Advanced (3+ years):** 16-24 sets/muscle/week

**Volume calculation example (chest):**
- Barbell Bench: 4 sets × 8 reps = 4 sets
- Dumbbell Incline: 3 sets × 10 reps = 3 sets
- Machine Press: 3 sets × 12 reps = 3 sets
- Cable Flys: 3 sets × 15 reps = 3 sets
- **Total: 13 sets (intermediate target met)**

**Progress:** Add 2-3 sets every 2-4 weeks if recovery adequate.

---

## Rep Ranges & Exercise Selection

**Primary rep range: 6-12 reps** (60-70% of volume)

- 60-70% volume at 8-12 reps (metabolic stress + mechanical tension)
- 20-30% volume at 6-8 reps (maximum tension, heavy)
- 10-20% volume at 12-15 reps (metabolic stress, light)

**Exercise hierarchy:**
1. **Compound movements first** (60-70% volume): Bench, squat, rows, OHP
2. **Isolation movements second** (30-40% volume): Flys, leg curls, lateral raises, isolation machines

**Why:** Compounds allow heavy loads + mechanical tension; isolations allow metabolic stress + damage.

---

## Rest Intervals

**By intensity:**
- Heavy (6-8 reps, 85%+ load): 2-3 minutes
- Moderate (8-12 reps, 70-80%): 90-120 seconds
- Light (12-15 reps, 60-70%): 45-60 seconds

**Principle:** Longer rest for heavy loads (CNS recovery); shorter rest for moderate loads (maintain metabolic stress).

---

## Training Frequency

**Optimal: 2x per week per muscle**

Example (chest):
- **Day A:** Heavy focus (5-6 reps, mechanical tension)
- **Day B:** Volume focus (10-12 reps, metabolic stress)

**Frequency advantages:** Better MPS timing; recover between sessions; allows exercise variation.

---

## Progression Strategy

**Progressive overload (in priority order):**

1. **Add weight:** +2.5-5 kg every 1-2 weeks
2. **Add reps:** If can't add weight, add reps until hitting target, then reset weight
3. **Add sets:** If stuck on both, add 1-2 sets per exercise
4. **Improve form:** Deeper ROM, better control

**Example:**
- Week 1: Bench 5 sets × 8 reps @ 80 kg
- Week 2: Bench 5 sets × 9 reps @ 80 kg (added rep)
- Week 3: Bench 5 sets × 8 reps @ 82.5 kg (reset reps, added weight)

---

## Time-Under-Tension (TUT)

**Target:** 40-70 seconds per set

**Tempo examples:**
- Heavy (6-8 reps): 2:0:2:0 = 2s up, 2s down = 32-48s total (acceptable)
- Moderate (8-12 reps): 2:1:3:1 = 2s up, 1s pause, 3s down, 1s pause = 60-72s total (optimal)
- Light (12-15 reps): 1:0:2:0 = 1s up, 2s down = 45-60s total (acceptable)

**Principle:** Emphasis on eccentric (lowering) phase (3+ seconds) increases mechanical tension and damage.

---

## Nutrition for Hypertrophy

**Protein:** 1.8-2.2 g/kg bodyweight daily

**Caloric surplus:** +300-500 kcal above maintenance

**Carbohydrates:** 4-7 g/kg (higher on training days)

**Fats:** 0.8-1.2 g/kg

**Example (80 kg person, 2500 kcal TDEE):**
- Target calories: 2500 + 400 = 2900 kcal
- Protein: 80 × 2.0 = 160g (640 kcal)
- Fats: 80 × 1.0 = 80g (720 kcal)
- Carbs: (2900 - 640 - 720) / 4 = 385g (1540 kcal)

---

## Recovery Priorities

1. **Sleep:** 7-9 hours per night
2. **Protein:** 160-176g daily (split across 4-5 meals)
3. **Hydration:** 3-4 liters daily
4. **Stress:** Minimize; daily relaxation practice
5. **Deload:** 1 week every 4-6 weeks (reduce volume 40-50%)

---

## Red Flags (Insufficient Hypertrophy)

- Volume <10 sets/muscle/week (too low)
- No progressive overload (same weight for weeks)
- Primary rep range outside 6-12 (wrong stimulus)
- Protein <1.6 g/kg (insufficient MPS)
- Sleep <6 hours/night (30-40% reduction in growth)
- Training same muscle daily (inadequate recovery)
- Excessive cardio (>5 hours/week; interferes with hypertrophy)

---

## Feedback Priorities for Reviews

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- Volume in target range (12-16 sets/muscle/week)
- Primary rep range 8-12 (60-70% of volume)
- Progressive overload visible (weight increased from last week)
- Compound + isolation mix (60/40 ratio)
- Rest intervals appropriate (90-120 sec for moderate intensity)
- Protein target on track (1.8+ g/kg logged)
- Recovery days present

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Volume too low (<10 sets/muscle/week)
- Excessive heavy (only 4-6 reps; missing metabolic stress)
- Excessive light (mostly 15+ reps; missing mechanical tension)
- No progressive overload (same weight repeatedly)
- Insufficient protein (below 1.6 g/kg)
- Sleep <6 hours (recovery bottleneck)
- No recovery days (training same muscle daily)
