# Fat Loss Training: Evidence-Based Goal-Specific Protocol

**Goal:** Maximize fat loss while preserving muscle mass through strategic training + nutrition.

**Sources:** ISSN, ACSM, Journal of Applied Physiology  
**Key principles:** Caloric deficit + Resistance training + Adequate protein + Cardio

---

## Training Philosophy for Fat Loss

**Critical principle:** Resistance training preserves muscle during deficit.

**Research (Helms et al., ISSN 2014):**
> During caloric deficit, resistance training is the primary stimulus for retaining muscle mass. Without resistance training, 30-50% of weight loss comes from muscle (bad). With resistance training, 80%+ of weight loss is fat (good).

**Training strategy:**
- Maintain heavy compound lifts (preserves muscle)
- Reduce accessory volume (save calories for cardio, recovery)
- Add strategic cardio (increases deficit without compromising resistance training)

---

## Resistance Training During Fat Loss

**Maintain strength = Maintain muscle**

**Primary focus:** Heavy compounds at high intensity

**Volume targets:** 8-12 sets per muscle per week (lower than hypertrophy)

- 60% volume at 6-8 reps (heavy, preserves muscle)
- 40% volume at 8-12 reps (moderate, metabolic stress)

**Example (chest):**
- Barbell Bench: 4 sets × 5-6 reps @ 82-85% 1RM
- Dumbbell Incline: 3 sets × 8 reps @ 75% 1RM
- Machine Press: 2 sets × 12 reps @ 65% 1RM
- **Total: 9 sets/week (adequate for preservation during deficit)**

**Key difference from hypertrophy:** Lower total volume (saves CNS energy for cardio).

---

## Caloric Deficit

**Optimal deficit for fat loss:** 500 kcal below TDEE

**Result:** ~0.5 kg fat loss per week

**Calculation example (80 kg person):**
- Estimated TDEE: 2400 kcal
- Target: 2400 - 500 = 1900 kcal/day
- Expected: 2 kg/week fat loss (0.5 kg × 7 days)

**Deficits by speed:**
- 250 kcal deficit: ~0.25 kg/week (slower, preserves more muscle)
- 500 kcal deficit: ~0.5 kg/week (moderate, good balance)
- 750+ kcal deficit: ~1 kg/week (aggressive, higher muscle loss risk)

**Recommendation:** 500 kcal is sweet spot; avoids excessive muscle loss while achieving visible progress.

---

## Protein During Fat Loss

**Protein requirement increases during deficit.**

**Target:** 2.0-2.4 g/kg bodyweight (higher than surplus)

**Why?** Deficit increases muscle breakdown; high protein activates mTOR to counteract this.

**Example (80 kg person):**
- At surplus: 160g protein/day sufficient
- At deficit: 160-192g protein/day needed (prioritize toward 2.0+ g/kg)

**Practical: Every meal should have 25-40g protein**

---

## Cardio for Fat Loss

**Cardio creates additional deficit without compromising strength training.**

**Optimal approach:** Low-intensity steady-state (LISS)

- **Duration:** 30-60 minutes
- **Intensity:** Zone 1-2 (60-70% max HR, conversational pace)
- **Frequency:** 3-5x per week
- **Type:** Walking, cycling, swimming, elliptical (joint-friendly)

**Why Zone 1-2?**
- Spares glycogen (preserves carbs for resistance training)
- Doesn't interfere with recovery
- Sustainable; low injury risk
- Allows social activity (walk with friend, watch show)

**Volume example:**
- Monday: 40 min zone 2 cycling
- Tuesday: Resistance training (heavy compounds)
- Wednesday: 30 min zone 2 walk
- Thursday: Resistance training (heavy compounds)
- Friday: 40 min zone 2 elliptical
- Sat-Sun: Rest or 30 min zone 1 walk

**Total: ~150-180 min/week cardio + 2 heavy training sessions**

## Caloric Intake Distribution

**Macro targets for 1900 kcal/day (80 kg person):**

- **Protein:** 160-192g (640-768 kcal, 34-40%)
- **Carbs:** 180-220g (720-880 kcal, 38-46%)
- **Fats:** 50-65g (450-585 kcal, 24-31%)

**Meal example (1900 kcal/day):**

**Breakfast (500 kcal):**
- Oatmeal (50g): 190 kcal, 5g protein, 35g carbs
- Whey protein shake (30g): 120 kcal, 25g protein
- Banana: 105 kcal, 1g protein, 27g carbs
- Peanut butter (1 tbsp): 95 kcal, 4g protein, 3g carbs, 8g fat

**Lunch (600 kcal):**
- Grilled chicken (180g): 280 kcal, 50g protein
- Brown rice (100g): 150 kcal, 3g protein, 33g carbs
- Broccoli + oil: 170 kcal, 3g protein, 15g carbs, 8g fat

**Snack (300 kcal):**
- Greek yogurt (200g): 150 kcal, 18g protein
- Berries + granola: 150 kcal, 2g protein, 30g carbs

**Dinner (500 kcal):**
- Lean beef (150g): 280 kcal, 40g protein, 10g fat
- Sweet potato (150g): 130 kcal, 2g protein, 30g carbs
- Olive oil (1 tbsp): 120 kcal, 14g fat

**Totals:** 1900 kcal, 165g protein (35%), 195g carbs (41%), 50g fat (24%)

## Metabolic Adaptation

**As you lose weight, TDEE decreases (fewer calories burned).**

**Mechanism:** Smaller body mass requires fewer calories at rest.

**Rule of thumb:** TDEE drops ~10 kcal per 1 kg bodyweight lost.

**Example:**
- Started at 80 kg, TDEE 2400 kcal
- Now 75 kg (lost 5 kg), new TDEE ~2350 kcal
- Old deficit: 2400 - 500 = 1900 kcal still works
- Or: Adjust to 2350 - 500 = 1850 kcal for same deficit

**Action:** Re-estimate TDEE every 4-6 weeks; adjust calorie target accordingly.

---

## Red Flags (Ineffective Fat Loss)

- Resistance training volume <8 sets/muscle/week (muscle loss risk)
- Excessive cardio (>300 min/week; interferes with recovery)
- Protein <1.6 g/kg (muscle breakdown accelerates)
- Deficit >750 kcal/day (excessive muscle loss, metabolic adaptation)
- No resistance training (20-30% of weight loss becomes muscle)
- Calorie counting inaccurate (underestimating food intake)
- Sleep <6 hours (increases hunger hormone; impairs deficit adherence)
- Extreme restriction (<1500 kcal/day; metabolic adaptation, unsustainable)

## Feedback Priorities for Reviews

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- Resistance training present (compounds prioritized)
- Adequate protein (160+ g/day for 80 kg person)
- Caloric deficit present (not excessive 250-500 kcal range)
- Cardio added (LISS 30-60 min, Zone 1-2)
- Heavy weights maintained (strength preserved = muscle preserved)
- Progressive tracking (weight, strength, measurements)
- Sleep adequate (7+ hours)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Minimal resistance training (cardio-only; muscle loss risk)
- Protein <1.6 g/kg (insufficient to preserve muscle)
- Excessive deficit (>750 kcal/day; accelerates muscle loss)
- Cardio volume too high (>300 min/week; interferes with resistance training)
- Strength declining significantly (indicates muscle loss)
- No calorie tracking (can't assess deficit)
- Sleep <6 hours (hunger hormone elevation; deficit non-adherence)
- Restrictive pattern (signs of unsustainable extreme diet)
