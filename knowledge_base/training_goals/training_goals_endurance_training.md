# Endurance Training: Evidence-Based Goal-Specific Protocol

**Goal:** Maximize aerobic capacity, VO2 max, and sustainable performance through polarized training.

**Sources:** ACSM, ISSN, Journal of Applied Physiology  
**Key principles:** 80/20 rule + Zone-based training + Long slow distance + Adequate recovery

---

## The 80/20 Rule (Polarized Training)

**Research consensus (Seiler & Kemi, 2011):**
> Elite endurance athletes spend 80% of training time in Zone 1-2 (easy) and 20% in Zone 4-5 (hard). Minimal time in Zone 3 (moderate).

**Why?**
- Zone 2 builds aerobic base with minimal fatigue
- Zone 3 causes fatigue without sufficient VO2 max stimulus
- Best progression: Separate easy and hard days; rarely train "moderate"

**Practical breakdown (weekly volume):**
- 80% = Zone 1-2 easy training
- 0% = Zone 3 moderate training (avoid)
- 20% = Zone 4-5 hard training

---

## Heart Rate Zones (Karvonen Formula)

**Zone 1 (Recovery):** 50-60% HRR
- Very easy; can hold conversation
- Used for: Active recovery, long easy runs
- No fatigue; sustainable indefinitely

**Zone 2 (Aerobic Base):** 60-70% HRR
- Comfortable; can speak in full sentences; slightly elevated breathing
- Used for: 60-70% of training volume
- Builds mitochondrial density, capillary density, fat oxidation
- Duration: 45-120+ minutes

**Zone 3 (Tempo):** 70-80% HRR
- Moderate; conversation becoming difficult
- AVOID for endurance training (high fatigue, insufficient stimulus)

**Zone 4 (Threshold):** 80-90% HRR
- Hard; can only speak single words
- Used for: 10-15% of training volume (1-2x per week)
- Improves lactate threshold
- Duration: 20-45 minutes total (intervals or continuous)

**Zone 5 (VO2 Max):** 90-100% HRR
- Maximum; anaerobic; very difficult
- Used for: 5-10% of training volume (1x per week maximum)
- Improves VO2 max
- Duration: 20-40 minutes total (intervals only)

---

## Weekly Training Structure

**Example week (running, 40 km):**

**Monday:** 8 km Zone 2 (easy aerobic base)

**Tuesday:** 5 km Zone 1 (recovery; active rest day)

**Wednesday:** 10 km with Zone 4 threshold work
- 2 km warm-up (Zone 1-2)
- 4 × 3 min @ Zone 4, 2 min easy recovery between
- 2 km cool-down (Zone 1)

**Thursday:** 6 km Zone 1 (recovery)

**Friday:** 12 km Zone 2 long run (Friday or Saturday; typically once/week)

**Saturday:** 5 km Zone 1 (easy)

**Sunday:** Rest day

**Total:** 
- Zone 1-2: 36 km (90% volume)
- Zone 4-5: 4 km (10% volume)
- Matches 80/20 rule

---

## Aerobic Base Building (Weeks 1-8)

**Focus:** Build fitness foundation before hard training.

**Structure:**
- Weeks 1-3: Zone 1-2 only; no hard intervals
- Weeks 4-6: Add one Zone 3-4 session; rest Zone 2
- Weeks 7-8: One Zone 4 + one Zone 5 session; rest Zone 2

**Example (running):**
- Week 1: 30 km all Zone 1-2
- Week 2: 35 km all Zone 1-2
- Week 3: 40 km all Zone 1-2
- Week 4: 35 km Zone 2 + 5 km Zone 4 (threshold work)
- Week 5: 40 km Zone 2 + 5 km Zone 4 + 3 km Zone 5
- Week 8: 40 km Zone 2 + 4 km Zone 4 + 3 km Zone 5

---

## Long Slow Distance (LSD)

**Definition:** Single longest training bout per week, performed easy (Zone 2).

**Distance targets:**
- Beginners: 45-60 minutes Zone 2
- Intermediate: 90-120 minutes Zone 2
- Advanced: 120-180+ minutes Zone 2

**Benefit:**
- Builds mitochondrial density (most important for endurance)
- Improves glycogen storage capacity
- Mental toughness
- Fat oxidation adaptation

**When:** Once per week; allow 5-7 days recovery before next hard session.

**Example (runner):**
- Monday-Friday: Easy runs or easy weeks
- Saturday: Long run 120 minutes Zone 2
- Sunday: Rest
- Monday-Thursday: Resume hard training

---

## VO2 Max Interval Sessions

**Research (Billat et al., 2000; Seiler, 2010):**
> Intervals at 90-100% VO2 max for 3-8 minutes are most effective for raising VO2 max.

**Session structure:**
- Warm-up: 10-15 minutes Zone 1-2
- Main set: 4-6 × 3-5 minutes @ Zone 5 (90%+ max HR)
  - 2-3 minutes easy recovery (Zone 1-2) between intervals
- Cool-down: 10 minutes Zone 1-2

**Example (runner):**
- 1 km easy warm-up
- 5 km total @ VO2 max (4 × 5 min intervals + recoveries)
- 1 km easy cool-down
- Total session: ~30 minutes, but only 20 minutes at or above threshold

**Frequency:** 1x per week maximum (very high fatigue; requires full recovery)

---

## Lactate Threshold / Tempo Sessions

**Definition:** Sustained efforts at lactate threshold (where lactate starts accumulating).

**Structure:**
- Warm-up: 10 minutes easy
- Main set: 2-3 × 8-10 minutes @ Zone 4
  - 2-3 minutes easy recovery between
- Cool-down: 10 minutes easy

**Example (runner):**
- 1 km easy warm-up
- 3 × 8 min @ Zone 4, 2 min easy between (24 min total hard)
- 1 km easy cool-down
- Total session: ~40 minutes

**Frequency:** 1-2x per week (less fatiguing than VO2 max)

---

## Weekly Mileage Progression

**Beginner:** 15-25 km/week

**Intermediate:** 30-60 km/week

**Advanced:** 60-100+ km/week

**The 10% Rule (injury prevention):**
> Increase weekly mileage by maximum 10% per week.

**Example (runner at 40 km/week):**
- Week 1: 40 km
- Week 2: 44 km (10% increase)
- Week 3: 48 km (9% increase)
- Week 4: 48 km (deload week, no increase)

**Consequence of violating rule:** Overuse injuries (stress fractures, tendinitis); 30-50% injury rate when exceeding 10%.

---

## Deload Weeks & Periodisation

**Deload frequency:** Every 4 weeks

**Deload structure:**
- Reduce volume by 40-50%
- Maintain intensity (keep hard sessions but fewer repeats)
- Example (running): 40 km → 20-24 km for week

**Deload week example:**
- Monday: 4 km Zone 2
- Tuesday: 4 km Zone 1
- Wednesday: 6 km with threshold work (but only 2 × 3 min instead of normal 3 × 8 min)
- Thursday: 3 km Zone 1
- Friday: 8 km Zone 2 (reduced long run)
- Sat-Sun: Rest
- **Total: 25 km (vs normal 40 km)**

**Purpose:** Recover from accumulated fatigue; prevent overtraining; allow adaptation.

---

## Metrics & Progression

**Resting Heart Rate (RHR):**
- Track daily
- Baseline: Average of 7-day RHR at start of training
- Improvement: RHR decreases 1-2 bpm per month with aerobic training
- Warning: RHR elevated 5+ bpm above baseline = incomplete recovery; reduce volume

**Example (runner):**
- Week 1 baseline: 62 bpm
- Week 4: 60 bpm (improving)
- Week 8: 58 bpm (continued improvement)

**Weekly Mileage Progression:**
- Week 1-3: 40 km
- Week 4: 28 km (deload)
- Week 5-7: 44 km
- Week 8: 30 km (deload)

**Velocity/Pace at Zone 2:**
- Track pace at same heart rate
- Faster pace at same HR = aerobic improvement
- Example: 9:30/km @ 150 bpm → 9:00/km @ 150 bpm (improved)

---

## Red Flags (Ineffective Endurance Training)

- Most sessions moderate intensity (Zone 3; insufficient easy-hard separation)
- Multiple hard sessions without adequate recovery (overtraining)
- Long run absent or too short (<60 min Zone 2)
- Excessive weekly mileage increase (>10% per week; injury risk)
- No deload weeks (accumulated fatigue impairs adaptation)
- RHR consistently elevated (inadequate recovery)
- Training 7 days/week (no recovery days; CNS fatigue)
- Excessive total volume (>200% of ability; unsustainable)

---

## Feedback Priorities for Reviews

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- 80%+ volume in Zone 1-2 (easy runs dominate)
- One dedicated hard session per week (Zone 4-5)
- Long run present (90+ min Zone 2, once/week)
- Mileage increases gradual (10% per week maximum)
- Weekly mileage consistent and sustainable
- Rest/recovery days present (2-3 per week minimum)
- RHR stable or improving
- Deload weeks scheduled every 4 weeks

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Most sessions moderate (Zone 3); insufficient easy-hard separation
- Multiple hard sessions without recovery (overtraining risk)
- Long run absent or too short (<60 min)
- Mileage jumps excessive (>10% per week; injury risk)
- Insufficient rest days (training 6-7 days/week)
- RHR elevated or rising (recovery inadequate)
- No visible periodisation or deload pattern
- Total volume appears unsustainable for fitness level
