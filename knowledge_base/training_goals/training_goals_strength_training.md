# Strength Training: Evidence-Based Goal-Specific Protocol

**Goal:** Maximum strength development (1RM progression) through heavy loads, neural adaptation, and periodisation.

**Sources:** NSCA, ACSM, Strength & Conditioning Journal  
**Key principles:** Heavy loads (85%+ 1RM) + Lower volume + Adequate recovery + Periodisation

---

## Training Volume Targets

**Weekly volume for competition lifts (squat, bench, deadlift):**

- **Beginners:** 4-6 sets/week per lift
- **Intermediate:** 6-10 sets/week per lift
- **Advanced:** 8-15 sets/week per lift

**Volume calculation (deadlift, intermediate):**
- Monday: 5 sets × 2-3 reps @ 90% = 5 sets
- Thursday: 3 sets × 5 reps @ 85% = 3 sets
- **Total: 8 sets/week (intermediate target met)**

**Key difference from hypertrophy:** Volume from multiple heavy singles/doubles, NOT high-rep sets.

**Accessory volume:** 4-8 sets/week (support lifts, weak points)

---

## Load & Rep Ranges

**Primary intensity: 85-90% 1RM** (60-70% of volume)

- Heavy (1-3 reps, 90%+ 1RM): Primary strength stimulus
- Moderate (3-5 reps, 85-90%): Strength + some hypertrophy
- Light (5-8 reps, 80-85%): Skill practice, recovery

**Volume distribution:**
- 60-70% at 85%+ 1RM (heavy singles/doubles)
- 20-30% at 80-85% 1RM (moderate triples/quintuples)
- 10% at 75-80% 1RM (lighter work, skill)

**Avoid:** High-rep sets (12+ reps) for main lifts; insufficient neural stimulus.

---

## Rest Intervals (Critical)

**For heavy loads (1-3 reps, 85%+ 1RM):** 3-5 minutes between sets

**Why?** ATP-PC system (phosphocreatine) fully recovers in 3 minutes; heavy singles require full system recovery.

**Insufficient rest consequences:**
- Reduced force production on subsequent sets (20-30% drop)
- Missed neural adaptations
- Increased fatigue without strength benefit

**Practical example:**
- Set 1: 3 reps @ 90% 1RM
- *Wait 3-5 minutes with light mobility*
- Set 2: 3 reps @ 90% 1RM (full recovery, same performance)

vs.

- Set 1: 3 reps @ 90% 1RM
- *Wait 60 seconds*
- Set 2: Only able to lift 85% 1RM (insufficient recovery)

---

## Exercise Selection Hierarchy

**Primary (60-70% volume):** Competition lifts
- Squat (back squat, front squat)
- Bench Press (barbell, variations)
- Deadlift (conventional, sumo)
- Overhead Press (standing barbell)

**Secondary (20-30%):** Close variations
- Pause squat, box squat
- Incline bench, close-grip bench, pin press
- Deficit deadlift, rack pull
- Seated OHP, dumbbell press

**Accessory (10%):** Support work
- Leg press, machine rows, lat pulldowns
- Arm work, core training
- These build weak points but lack specificity

**Principle:** 70% volume from competition lifts; don't get sidetracked with excessive accessory work.

---

## Training Frequency

**Optimal: 2-3x per week per lift**

**Example: Squat 2x/week**
- Monday: Heavy day (5 sets × 2-3 @ 90%)
- Thursday: Volume day (3 sets × 5 @ 85%)

**Why 2x/week:**
- Better recovery between sessions (48-72h)
- Neural stimulus repeated twice with full recovery
- 1x/week is too infrequent for optimal adaptation
- 3-4x/week leads to excessive fatigue without additional benefit

---

## Periodisation Models

### Linear Periodisation (Simplest)

**4-week cycle:**
- Week 1: 4 × 5 @ 80% 1RM
- Week 2: 4 × 4 @ 83% 1RM
- Week 3: 4 × 3 @ 86% 1RM
- Week 4: 4 × 2 @ 90% 1RM (peak)
- Week 5: Deload (50% volume)

### Wave Loading (Better recovery management)

**Per week (same movement trained 2x):**
- Day A: 5 × 3 @ 90% (heavy)
- Day B: 4 × 5 @ 85% (moderate)

Repeat weekly; every 3 weeks, increase load 2-5%.

---

## Progressive Overload Strategy

**Strength gains require consistent load progression.**

**Progression priority:**
1. **Add weight:** +2.5-5 kg every 1-2 weeks
2. **Add reps at same load:** If can't add weight, do extra rep at current weight
3. **Test new 1RM:** Every 4-6 weeks to verify progress

**Example (squat):**
- Week 1: 5 × 2 @ 180 kg
- Week 2: 5 × 2 @ 182.5 kg (added weight)
- Week 3: 5 × 2 @ 185 kg (added weight)
- Week 4: Test new 1RM (should be ~190-195 kg)

---

## Recovery Imperatives

**Strength training stresses CNS more than muscle fatigue.**

1. **Sleep:** 8-9 hours per night (CNS recovery)
2. **Between-session recovery:** 48-72 hours before heavy session on same lift
3. **Deload weeks:** Every 4-6 weeks (reduce volume 40-50%)
4. **Adequate calories:** Don't diet while pursuing strength (need energy for CNS recovery)
5. **Stress management:** High stress elevates cortisol (interferes with CNS recovery)

---

## Nutrition for Strength

**Protein:** 1.6-2.0 g/kg (same as hypertrophy; no additional benefit above)

**Calories:** Maintenance or slight surplus

**Carbohydrates:** 3-4 g/kg (adequate for training volume)

**Caffeine:** 3-6 mg/kg (improves CNS function, performance; take 30-45 min pre-workout)

**Creatine:** 3-5 g/day (improves ATP-PC system recovery, slight strength gain of 2-3%)

---

## Red Flags (Insufficient Strength Progression)

- Load <80% 1RM for primary sets (insufficient neural stimulus)
- Volume >15 sets/week per lift (excessive fatigue without additional gains)
- Rest intervals <2 minutes for heavy singles (incomplete CNS recovery)
- Training same lift 3+ days/week without adequate recovery
- No 1RM test in 8+ weeks (can't track progress)
- Poor sleep (<6 hours; CNS cannot recover)
- Excessive accessory work (more than 30% volume)
- No deload weeks (accumulated fatigue impairs adaptation)

---

## Feedback Priorities for Reviews

✅ **Excellent indicators:**
- Primary lifts used (squat, bench, deadlift focus)
- Heavy load (85%+ perceived 1RM for main sets)
- Appropriate volume (6-10 sets/lift/week)
- Long rest intervals (3-5 min between heavy sets)
- Progressive overload (load increased from last week)
- Deload week scheduled (every 4-6 weeks)
- Recovery emphasis (adequate sleep, days between sessions)

⚠️ **Areas needing improvement:**
- Load too light (60-75% 1RM; insufficient neural stimulus)
- Excessive volume (15+ heavy sets/week; impairs recovery)
- Insufficient rest (resting <2 min between heavy doubles)
- Missing main competition lifts (only accessories)
- No progressive overload visible (same weight for weeks)
- Training same lift daily without recovery
- No periodic deload weeks (fatigue accumulation)
- Sleep <7 hours/night (CNS recovery bottleneck)
