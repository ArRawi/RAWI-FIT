## PART 1 — STRENGTH STANDARDS BY BODY WEIGHT

### How to Use

1. Calculate `lift_kg / body_weight_kg` = **relative strength ratio**
2. Compare ratio to the table for the relevant lift and sex
3. Assign level: Beginner → Novice → Intermediate → Advanced → Elite

**Important caveats:**
- Standards are guidelines, not absolute measures
- Limb length, muscle insertions, and age affect performance independent of effort
- Use to track your own progress over time — not to judge others
- Female upper-body standards are 50–65% of male; lower-body standards are 70–85%

> Source: ExRx.net, Strength Level, BiteKit Strength Standards, Arvo Strength Level Calculator (2026)

### 1A. Back Squat Standards (Bodyweight Multipliers)

| Level | Male × BW | Female × BW | Training Age |
|---|---|---|---|
| Beginner | < 0.75× | < 0.5× | < 6 months |
| Novice | 0.75–1.25× | 0.5–0.75× | 6–18 months |
| Intermediate | 1.25–1.75× | 0.75–1.1× | 1.5–3 years |
| Advanced | 1.75–2.25× | 1.1–1.5× | 3–6 years |
| Elite | > 2.25× | > 1.5× | 6–10+ years |

**Example:** 80 kg male squats 120 kg → 120/80 = 1.5× → **Intermediate**

### 1E. Strength Balance Ratios (Cross-Lift)

Useful for detecting weak points. Based on a typical intermediate male lifter:

| Ratio | Target | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Squat : Deadlift | Squat = 80–90% of deadlift | Squat much weaker → work quad strength + technique |
| Bench : Squat | Bench = 65–75% of squat | Bench much stronger → add leg work |
| OHP : Bench | OHP = 60–70% of bench | OHP lagging → add vertical pressing volume |
| Pull-Up : Bodyweight | 8+ reps = intermediate | Below 5 reps → prioritize pull-up progression |

## PART 2 — CARDIOVASCULAR FITNESS BENCHMARKS

### 2A. VO2 Max Norms by Age and Sex

VO2 max = maximum oxygen uptake (ml O2 per kg body weight per min). Single best predictor of aerobic fitness and longevity.

> "Cardiorespiratory fitness, measured by VO2 max, was inversely associated with all-cause mortality at every level. The least fit group faced a mortality risk comparable to that of smokers." — Cleveland Clinic / JAMA Network Open (2018)

#### VO2 Max Classification — Males (ml/kg/min)

| Age | Very Poor | Poor | Fair | Good | Excellent | Superior |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 20–29 | < 25 | 25–33 | 34–42 | 43–52 | 53–62 | > 62 |
| 30–39 | < 23 | 23–30 | 31–38 | 39–48 | 49–57 | > 57 |
| 40–49 | < 20 | 20–26 | 27–35 | 36–44 | 45–53 | > 53 |
| 50–59 | < 18 | 18–24 | 25–33 | 34–42 | 43–50 | > 50 |
| 60–69 | < 16 | 16–22 | 23–30 | 31–38 | 39–48 | > 48 |
| 70+ | < 14 | 14–20 | 21–27 | 28–35 | 36–44 | > 44 |

#### VO2 Max Classification — Females (ml/kg/min)

| Age | Very Poor | Poor | Fair | Good | Excellent | Superior |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 20–29 | < 24 | 24–30 | 31–37 | 38–48 | 49–56 | > 56 |
| 30–39 | < 20 | 20–27 | 28–33 | 34–44 | 45–52 | > 52 |
| 40–49 | < 17 | 17–23 | 24–30 | 31–41 | 42–49 | > 49 |
| 50–59 | < 15 | 15–20 | 21–27 | 28–37 | 38–45 | > 45 |
| 60–69 | < 13 | 13–17 | 18–23 | 24–34 | 35–43 | > 43 |
| 70+ | < 11 | 11–15 | 16–22 | 23–31 | 32–40 | > 40 |

> Source: ACSM Fitness Classifications (2021); Cooper Institute VO2 Max Norms; BodySpec

**Health minimum targets:**
- Men: ≥ 35–40 ml/kg/min associated with reduced heart disease risk
- Women: ≥ 27–31 ml/kg/min associated with reduced heart disease risk

> Source: ACSM Guidelines (2021); BodySpec

---

### 2B. VO2 Max Decline with Age

| Decade | Average VO2 Max Decline |
|---|---|
| Per decade (sedentary) | ~10% |
| Per year (sedentary) | ~1% |
| Per year (trained) | ~0.5% |

VO2 max peaks around age 25–30, then declines. Regular aerobic training significantly slows this decline.

**VO2 Max Estimation Without Lab Test:**

Wearable estimate (Apple Watch, Garmin, Polar) — accuracy within 5–15% of true VO2 max. Useful for trend tracking even if absolute values are estimates.

---

### 2C. Resting Heart Rate (RHR) Norms

Lower resting heart rate = more efficient heart = better cardiovascular fitness.

| RHR (bpm) | Classification |
|---|---|
| < 40 | Athletic / Elite |
| 40–50 | Very Good / Well-trained |
| 51–60 | Good |
| 61–70 | Normal / Average |
| 71–80 | Below Average |
| 81–90 | Poor |
| > 90 | Very Poor / Potential concern |

**Normal range:** 60–100 bpm (AHA). Athletes often 40–60 bpm.

RHR decreases with consistent aerobic training — a dropping RHR over weeks/months is a reliable progress indicator.

---

### 2D. Heart Rate Recovery (HRR)

How quickly HR drops in the 1–2 minutes after peak exercise. Better recovery = better fitness.

| HRR After 1 Minute | Classification |
|---|---|
| > 25 bpm drop | Excellent |
| 20–25 bpm drop | Good |
| 15–20 bpm drop | Average |
| 12–15 bpm drop | Below Average |
| < 12 bpm drop | Poor / Potential cardiac concern |

> A drop of < 12 bpm at 1 min post-exercise is associated with increased cardiovascular mortality risk (NSCA; AHA).

## PART 5 — PROGRESS TRACKING LOGIC FOR APP

### Strength Progress Signals

| Signal | What It Means | App Feedback |
|---|---|---|
| Weight increased on any set vs last session | Progressing — overload achieved | ✅ "Progressing on [exercise]" |
| Reps increased at same weight | Progressing — volume overload | ✅ "Added reps — try +weight next session" |
| Same weight and reps as last 3 sessions | Stalled | ⚠️ "No progression on [exercise] for 3 sessions" |
| Weight decreased from previous session | Regression | 🔍 "Weight dropped — check recovery, sleep, nutrition" |
| New exercise attempted (no prior data) | Baseline established | "Baseline set — aim to improve next session" |

---

### Cardio Progress Signals

| Signal | Meaning | App Feedback |
|---|---|---|
| Same pace at lower HR than previous session | Aerobic fitness improving | ✅ "Zone 2 efficiency improving" |
| HR recovered faster post-session | Cardiovascular fitness improving | ✅ "Heart rate recovery improving" |
| Same HR but longer duration achieved | Endurance progressing | ✅ |
| HR spiking higher than usual at same pace | Fatigue, dehydration, or illness | ⚠️ "Elevated HR at familiar effort — check sleep/hydration" |
| VO2 max estimate declining (wearable) | Possible overtraining or deconditioning | ⚠️ "VO2 max trending down — review training balance" |

### Strength Level Tier Check

```
INPUT: exercise, weight_lifted_kg, body_weight_kg, sex

STEP 1: Calculate ratio = weight_lifted_kg / body_weight_kg

STEP 2: Look up ratio in standards table (Part 1A–1D) for the exercise and sex

STEP 3: Assign tier (Beginner / Novice / Intermediate / Advanced / Elite)

STEP 4: Calculate distance to next tier
  next_tier_ratio = next level × body_weight_kg
  gap = next_tier_ratio − weight_lifted_kg

OUTPUT: "Your [exercise] is at [Tier] level.
  You need [gap] kg to reach [Next Tier].
  Estimated time: [X] months at current progression rate."
```
