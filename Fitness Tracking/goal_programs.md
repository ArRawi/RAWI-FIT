# Goal-Based Training Programs
## Fitness Tracker App — Knowledge Base File 5 of 8

Structured week-by-week program templates for each major fitness goal.
Use these to generate plans, evaluate if a user's logged workouts match their goal, and suggest next steps.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE

1. **Program selection** — match user's goal + experience level to the right template
2. **Compliance check** — compare user's logged sessions to what the program prescribes
3. **Progression tracking** — detect when to advance the user to the next phase or level
4. **Plan generation** — use templates to suggest structured weekly schedules

---

## PART 1 — EXPERIENCE LEVEL DEFINITIONS

| Level | Training Age | Typical Strength Level | Progression Speed |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Beginner** | 0–6 months consistent training | Below novice standards | Weekly (session-to-session) |
| **Novice** | 6–18 months | Approaching novice standards | Weekly |
| **Intermediate** | 1.5–3 years structured training | Intermediate standards on major lifts | Monthly |
| **Advanced** | 3–6+ years | Advanced standards; periodized programming | Quarterly / Block-based |
| **Elite** | 6–10+ years + competitive | Elite standards | Annual planning |

> Most app users will be Beginner → Intermediate. Advanced+ users benefit from coach-designed programs.

## PART 2 — GOAL: GENERAL FITNESS & HEALTH

### Program: Full-Body 3x/Week (Beginner)

**Best for:** New to training, returning after 6+ months off, general health goal
**Frequency:** 3 days/week (e.g. Mon/Wed/Fri)
**Session duration:** 45–60 min
**Source framework:** ACSM General Health Guidelines; WHO 150 min/week minimum

#### Weekly Structure

| Day | Focus | Key Movements |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Full Body A | Squat, Push (horizontal), Pull (vertical), Hinge, Core |
| Day 2 | Rest or light cardio | Zone 1–2 walk, 20–30 min |
| Day 3 | Full Body B | Hinge, Push (vertical), Pull (horizontal), Squat variation, Core |
| Day 4 | Rest or light cardio | Zone 1–2 activity |
| Day 5 | Full Body A | Repeat Day 1 with slight progression |
| Day 6–7 | Rest / active recovery | Walks, stretching, mobility |

#### Session Template (Full Body A)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Goblet Squat or Back Squat | 3 × 10–12 | 90 sec | Learn squat pattern first |
| Dumbbell Bench Press | 3 × 10–12 | 90 sec | Or push-ups if no equipment |
| Lat Pulldown or Assisted Pull-Up | 3 × 10–12 | 90 sec | Pull-up regression acceptable |
| Romanian Deadlift | 3 × 10–12 | 90 sec | Hip hinge pattern |
| Plank | 3 × 20–30 sec | 60 sec | Build to 60 sec |

**Progression rule:** Add 2.5 kg (lower body) or 1.25 kg (upper body) when all sets completed with good form. Add reps first if weight jumps feel too large.

### Program A: Full-Body Hypertrophy 3x/Week (Beginner–Novice)

**Best for:** < 1 year training; hypertrophy goal
**Volume target:** 10–16 sets per muscle per week (spread across 3 sessions)
**Rep range:** 8–15
**Source:** ACSM 2026 Position Stand; NSCA hypertrophy guidelines

## PART 4 — GOAL: STRENGTH

### Program: Linear Progression (Beginner–Novice Strength)

**Best for:** Beginners focused on strength; 0–18 months training
**Core principle:** Add weight every session (or every week)
**Frequency:** 3x/week full body
**Rep range:** 3–6 (primary lifts) + 8–15 (accessories)
**Source:** NSCA strength training principles; Starting Strength / StrongLifts methodology

#### Weekly Structure (Alternating A/B)

| Week Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Monday | Session A |
| Wednesday | Session B |
| Friday | Session A (Week 1) / B (Week 2) |

#### Session A

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Progression |
|---|---|---|
| Back Squat | 3 × 5 | +2.5 kg every session |
| Bench Press | 3 × 5 | +1.25–2.5 kg every session |
| Deadlift | 1 × 5 (heavy) | +2.5–5 kg every session |
| Pull-Up / Lat Pulldown | 3 × 5–8 | Add reps or weight each session |

#### Session B

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Progression |
|---|---|---|
| Back Squat | 3 × 5 | +2.5 kg every session |
| Overhead Press | 3 × 5 | +1.25 kg every session |
| Deadlift or Romanian Deadlift | 3 × 5 | Alternate with deadlift |
| Barbell Row | 3 × 5 | +1.25–2.5 kg every session |

**When to stop linear progression:** When you fail to add weight for 3 consecutive sessions on the same lift → switch to weekly or block-based progression.

---

### Program: Intermediate Strength — 5/3/1-Style Weekly Progression

**Best for:** 1.5–4 years training; stalled on linear progression
**Principle:** 4-week waves; each week has a different intensity/rep focus
**Source:** Based on Wendler 5/3/1 methodology; NSCA periodization principles

#### 4-Week Wave Structure (per main lift)

| Week | Sets × Reps | % 1RM |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | 3 × 5 | 65 / 75 / 85% |
| Week 2 | 3 × 3 | 70 / 80 / 90% |
| Week 3 | 5/3/1 | 75 / 85 / 95% |
| Week 4 | Deload | 40 / 50 / 60% (3 × 5) |

**Main lifts:** Squat, Deadlift, Bench Press, Overhead Press (one per session)
**After each 4-week wave:** Add 2.5–5 kg to upper body training max; 5 kg to lower body

## PART 5 — GOAL: FAT LOSS

### Program: Fat Loss Resistance + Cardio Split

**Best for:** Any level; primary goal is fat loss while preserving muscle
**Key principle:** Maintain resistance training intensity; do not dramatically cut volume
**Source:** ISSN; NSCA; ACSM (preserving lean mass during caloric deficit requires resistance training)

#### Weekly Structure

| Day | Activity | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Resistance Training (Full Body or Upper) | Maintain intensity; compound focus |
| Tuesday | Cardio Zone 2 (30–45 min) | Brisk walk, cycling, swimming |
| Wednesday | Resistance Training (Full Body or Lower) | |
| Thursday | Cardio Zone 2–3 (30 min) or HIIT (20 min) | HIIT max 2x/week |
| Friday | Resistance Training (Full Body or Upper) | |
| Saturday | Long Zone 2 cardio (45–60 min) | Steady state; fat oxidation |
| Sunday | Rest / gentle walk | |

**Cardio first or lifting first?** If doing both same day, lift first — strength performance drops significantly after cardio.

**HIIT protocol example:**
```
Warm-up: 5 min Zone 1
Work:     20–40 sec at Zone 4–5 effort (sprint, bike, row)
Rest:     40–80 sec at Zone 1
Rounds:   8–12 rounds
Cool-down: 5 min Zone 1
Total: 20–30 min
```

**Key nutrition note:** See `nutrition_and_fueling.md` — maintain 300–500 kcal deficit; protein at 2.2–3.1 g/kg during cut.

## PART 6 — GOAL: CALISTHENICS SKILL PROGRESSION

### Program: Calisthenics Foundation to Skills

**Best for:** Any level; bodyweight-only or calisthenics-focused goal
**Principle:** Master prerequisites before attempting advanced skills
**Source:** Based on established calisthenics skill ladder progressions

#### Skill Prerequisite Map

```
PULL-UP PROGRESSION:
Dead Hang → Scapular Pull → Negative Pull-Up → Band-Assisted Pull-Up
→ Pull-Up (3×5) → Weighted Pull-Up → Archer Pull-Up → One-Arm Negative → One-Arm Pull-Up

PUSH-UP / PLANCHE PROGRESSION:
Wall Push-Up → Knee Push-Up → Push-Up (3×10) → Decline Push-Up
→ Archer Push-Up → Pseudo-Planche Push-Up → Tuck Planche Hold → Planche

HANDSTAND PROGRESSION:
Pike Push-Up → Elevated Pike Push-Up → Wall Handstand Hold
→ Freestanding Handstand (5 sec) → Handstand Push-Up (wall) → Freestanding HSPU

L-SIT PROGRESSION:
Tuck Sit (floor/parallettes) → L-Sit Leg Raise Negative
→ L-Sit Hold (3 sec) → L-Sit Hold (10 sec) → V-Sit

PISTOL SQUAT PROGRESSION:
Box Squat (single leg) → Assisted Pistol (hold support)
→ Pistol to Box → Full Pistol Squat
```

#### Weekly Structure (Beginner Calisthenics)

| Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Monday | Push + Core |
| Tuesday | Rest or active recovery |
| Wednesday | Pull + Legs |
| Thursday | Rest or light cardio |
| Friday | Skill practice + Full Body |
| Saturday–Sunday | Rest |

#### Progression Rules for Calisthenics

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Can do 3 × 10 reps clean | Move to next progression |
| Cannot do 5 reps | Stay at current level; do negatives/partials |
| Skill hold < 5 sec | Work entry skill; don't skip prerequisites |
| Any pain (not muscle burn) | Stop; address with mobility and regression |

## PART 7 — GOAL: CARDIOVASCULAR FITNESS & ENDURANCE

### Program: Run/Cardio Base Building

**Best for:** Improving cardio fitness; Zone 2 base; half-marathon / 5K prep
**Principle:** 80% of training in Zone 1–2; 20% in Zone 3–5 (80/20 rule)
**Source:** BarBell Medicine; VO2 Master; endurance sports science

#### 8-Week Base Building Block

| Week | Zone 1–2 Volume | Zone 3–5 Work | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | 3 × 30 min | 1 × 15 min Zone 3 | Establish consistency |
| 3–4 | 3 × 35 min | 1 × 20 min Zone 3 | Build volume |
| 5–6 | 3 × 40 min | 1 × 20 min Zone 3 + 1 × 15 min intervals | Introduce Zone 4 |
| 7 | 3 × 40 min | 1 × 25 min mixed Zone 3–4 | Volume peak |
| 8 | 2 × 25 min (easy) | None | Deload week |

#### Weekly Cardio Template (Maintenance Phase)

| Day | Session | Zone | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | Easy run / bike / swim | Zone 2 | 30–40 min |
| Tuesday | Rest or strength training | — | — |
| Wednesday | Interval session | Zone 4–5 intervals + Zone 1 recovery | 25–30 min total |
| Thursday | Easy recovery cardio | Zone 1 | 20–30 min |
| Friday | Moderate steady state | Zone 3 | 30–40 min |
| Saturday | Long easy session | Zone 1–2 | 45–60 min |
| Sunday | Rest | — | — |

## PART 8 — PROGRAM COMPLIANCE REVIEW LOGIC

Use this to evaluate whether a user's logged workouts match their selected program.

| Check | Pass Condition | Flag Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Session count matches | Logged sessions = prescribed frequency ± 1 | Logged < 70% of prescribed sessions |
| Correct movement patterns | Key compound patterns present (SQUAT, HINGE, PUSH, PULL) | Missing any fundamental pattern in 2+ sessions |
| Volume on track | Sets per muscle within ±20% of program target | Consistently under or over by > 20% |
| Progressive overload | Any improvement vs last session (reps or weight) | Same numbers for 3+ sessions on same lift |
| Rest days respected | No same muscle group within 48h | Same muscle hit on consecutive days |
| Cardio zones appropriate | Zone logged matches program prescription | Zone 4–5 every session (no base building) |
| Deload taken | Deload week completed every 4–8 weeks | No deload in 8+ weeks of high-intensity training |

## PART 9 — PROGRAM ADVANCEMENT TRIGGERS

| Signal | Action |
|---|---|
| Linear progression stalling (3 consecutive fails) | Advance to weekly progression; suggest deload first |
| All beginner standards achieved | Suggest intermediate program |
| User logging > prescribed volume consistently | Suggest advancing to higher-volume program |
| User missing sessions regularly | Suggest reducing frequency; lower-commitment program |
| Cardio performance plateauing at Zone 2 | Add 1 Zone 4 interval session per week |
| Calisthenics skill prerequisite met (3×10 clean) | Unlock next progression in skill ladder |
